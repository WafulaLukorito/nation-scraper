# ICC forum put off over State doubts

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2011-12-15T11:29:47Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/news/politics/icc-forum-put-off-over-state-doubts-793154  

---

The Kenyan Government reservations about a planned New York forum featuring top ICC officials have contributed to a postponement.

Kenya’s envoy to the United Nations Macharia Kamau said on Tuesday that the country’s representatives had not been asked to play a role in organising the discussion on the topic “Peace and Justice in Kenya in 2012: How Kenyans in the Diaspora Can Contribute.”

Mr Kamau said he had made his views known to ICC Chief Prosecutor Luis Moreno-Ocampo on the sidelines of an ICC conference in New York on Monday. Mr Moreno-Ocampo, along with his designated successor, Fatou Bensouda, were to speak at the December 16 forum at the Manhattan offices of the New York City Bar Association.

The prosecutor’s office announced that it is postponing the event.

The discussion may now be held in January, added the notice from Shamiso Mbizvo, the cooperation advisor for the Kenyan teams in the ICC prosecutor’s office. The prosecutor’s office did not respond to requests for comments on the postponement.

Prof Makau Mutua, dean of the law school at a branch of the State University of New York, said the ICC was acting within its mandate by organising a public discussion.

A number of Kenyans believe that the ICC’s prosecution of six prominent figures is “politically motivated,” he continued and this would have “explain why this is not so".

Prof Mutua said he had intended to point out that the ICC had taken on the six cases “because the courts in Nairobi had not been able to do so".

He indicated he would also have said “our sympathies should be with the victims of the 2007 post-election violence”.
