## What you need to know:

* Governor Alfred Mutua signed into law the new Finance Act in October 16 last year.

Machakos Senator Johnstone Muthama has told residents to desist from paying the new taxes imposed by the county government.

“Why do you want to pay levies for people who are wasting your money flying around the world in pretext of working?” posed Mr Muthama.

Governor Alfred Mutua signed into law the new Finance Act in October 16 last year.

(READ: [Controversy over Machakos county taxes](http://www.nation.co.ke/counties/Machakos-County-Taxes-Protest-Mombasa-Road/-/1107872/2144372/-/25tsqbz/-/index.html))

He chastised the county government for introducing taxes for burials, slaughtering livestock, selling vegetables and bodaboda operation.

Two MPS Patrick Makau (Mavoko) and Mr Robert Mbui ( Kathiani) also backed the Senator.

According to the three leaders, the new levies were as a result of overspending by the county government.

“You should not allow the county government to tax you the poor heavily when some rich industrialists here do not pay taxes,” said Muthama.

**COURT CASE**

This comes as a civil case against the new taxes was moved from the Nairobi High Court to Machakos.

The case was filed in December last year by the applicants through their chairman Mr Simon Kitheka.

During Thursday's court session, lawyer for the county government Wilfred Nyamu asked to be given time to prepare his defence.

Justice Mutende granted him seven days to file a response on Dr Khaminwa's case against the county government.

The Senator also dismissed a move by some members of Parliament lobbying to have him removed from office said that he will continue fighting for the poor and ensure development is realised in the county.
