# Koigi should return to the right side of history

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2010-06-26T13:05:15Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/koigi-should-return-to-the-right-side-of-history-638950  

---

Is Koigi Wamwere a reformer, or simply a contrarian by nature? His political life is a history of contradictions. He purports to organise the masses, but he’s a lone ranger.

He claims to be a reformer, yet he finds common cause with dangerous kleptocrats. He rails against tribalism, but seems to believe in Kikuyu exceptionalism. He suffered enormous torment under the one-party dictatorship, yet he became an apologist for the corrupt Narc regime he served as a junior minister.

Most puzzling, Mr Wamwere has become a crusader against the new democratic constitution. Is Mr Wamwere an egocentric opportunist, or a committed individualist who prefers to paddle his own boat?

Mr Wamwere has a very high opinion of himself. He’s a bright guy with enormous courage. But megalomania is his Achilles heel. An insufferable attention seeker, he will make you notice him if you overlook him. Mr Wamwere has published quixotic books in which he’s often the moving spirit. He can’t stand being out of the national limelight. Like other eccentrics, he must be at the centre of everything. That’s why I don’t think Mr Wamwere’s opposition to the draft constitution is principled. He is licking his chops after splitting with PNU and losing in the 2007 elections. His opposition to the proposed constitution is out of pique because he feels left out.

Understand that I am not trying to knock Mr Wamwere. The man has toiled in the vineyards and earned his epaulettes. He opposed the repressive state of Mzee Jomo Kenyatta and Attorney-General Charles Njonjo.

They – and then Vice-President Daniel arap Moi – threw him in the dungeon for three years in 1975. With other legislators – Abuya Abuya, James Orengo, Chelagat Mutai, Chibule wa Tsuma, Mashengu wa Mwachofi, and Lawrence Sifuna – Mr Wamwere was one of the “Seven Bearded Sisters” who formed the unofficial opposition to Mr Moi’s repressive one-party state. He was detained after the 1982 coup attempt. He was in and out of detention and jail between 1990 and1996.

Mr Wamwere is a true hero of the second liberation. He sacrificed more dearly than most for the freedoms Kenyans enjoy today. To Kanu, he was public enemy number one. But, like many other movement heroes, Mr Wamwere has conflated his sacrifice with an entitlement to lead. That’s why it’s embarrassing to watch him try to elbow his way to the front by clutching on reeds. I know he desperately seeks relevance. But there are nobler ways of being a patriot than engaging in pitiable charades. His case vindicates the truism that not all publicity is good. Nor am I trying to muzzle him – he has every right to speak. But “elders of the struggle” need to comport themselves with decorum.

I am troubled by Mr Wamwere’s opposition to the proposed constitution. The constitution contains everything – and more – that he fought for in the last four decades. He argues – erroneously – that the constitution brings back majimbo. What utter and complete balderdash! He has joined Mr Moi, Higher Education minister William Ruto, Canon Peter Karanja, and John Cardinal Njue in an unholy alliance to oppose the proposed constitution.

But, unlike Mr Wamwere, Mr Moi opposes the document because it doesn’t provide for majimbo! That’s why I fail to see why Mr Wamwere is in bed with Mr Moi. Doesn’t he see through his own hypocrisy? Why would Mr Wamwere join the most rabid anti-reformers to oppose the most democratic constitution in the country’s history?

I take issue with one of Mr Wamwere’s central themes. I am talking about his Johnny one note on “negative ethnicity”. His use of this term makes me doubt his commitment to a democratic Kenya in which people are judged not by their ethnicity, but by “the content of their character’’. I find the concept uncompelling and vacuous. Ethnicity is neither “negative” nor “positive”. It just is. I suspect that Mr Wamwere wants to talk about “tribalism” but opts for “negative ethnicity” because it allows him to justify “tribalism” under the banner of “positive ethnicity”. I don’t buy the rhetorical ploy. “Positive ethnicity” is a semantic subterfuge for tribalism. He should also drop the alarmist talk of genocide.

Mr Wamwere has served his time for Kenya. He was detained, jailed, and exiled for over 20 years. That’s a lifetime. I cannot think of many Kenyans who have suffered so much for the rest of us to be free. Perhaps Mr Wamwere would handle this legacy of persecution more nobly if the State and society acknowledged their debt to him. Regrettably, Kenya has a history of forgetting – and even spiting – its heroes and heroines.

That’s the fate of the Mau Mau. It’s the result of the conservative elite that rules Kenya. Ironically, Mr Wamwere is in cahoots with the same anti-reform elite – Mr Moi and his ilk – that would erase him from the national memory.

My hope is that Mr Wamwere will see the error of his ways and quickly return to the right side of history. He must support the proposed constitution because it is a vindication of his life’s decorated struggle for freedom.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC*
