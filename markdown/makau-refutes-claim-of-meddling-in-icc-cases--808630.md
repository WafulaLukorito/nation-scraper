# Makau refutes claim of meddling in ICC cases 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2012-04-17T19:33:17Z

---

## What you need to know:

* Human rights activist accuses police of trying to shut him up

A prominent law professor and human rights activist on Tuesday refuted police claims that he interfered with International Criminal Court witnesses.

Prof Makau Mutua, one of the founders of the Kenya Human Rights Commission and dean of a leading American law school, accused police of trying to gag him.

The Criminal Investigations Department has asked Prof Mutua and Mr Maina Kiai, former chairman of the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights, to record statements over claims of witness tampering.

“The investigation is all about tampering with witnesses, not any other allegations,” CID director Ndegwa Muhoro said.

Also required for questioning is a former spokesman of Ugandan rebel leader Joseph Kony, Dr David Matsanga.

Mr Paul Muite, representing Mr Kiai and Prof Mutua, appeared before detectives on Tuesday.

The investigation in particular involves “Witness No. 4,” who is to testify against Mr Uhuru Kenyatta and Mr Francis Muthaura.

Dr Matsanga complained that articles authored by Prof Makau and Mr Kiai in the Sunday Nation and Daily Nation had interfered with the witness.

He released videos and statements purported to have been obtained from the witness saying he no longer wished to cooperate with the ICC.

Mr Muite told investigating officer John Kariuki that his clients would not record statements.

“There was no criminal act committed. I told them that I cannot see how there can be blackmail, incitement or interference with ICC witnesses. I told them that I had advised my clients so.It’s a waste of taxpayers’ money to investigate my clients,” he said.

Prof Makau was contacted by a CID officer over a complaint by a Chief Charles A Taku, representing Dr Matsanga.

Prof Mutua said: “The message did not disclose any law that I was alleged to have broken, except make unintelligible references to vague offences.

“Why the CID would waste scarce resources on such a wild goose chase beats me,” he said.

He described as “a sham” the investigation and said it “is part of an orchestrated campaign to intimidate and harass those who support the ICC process, the press, and thwart justice for victims. My message to the CID and those on whose behalf they seek to silence me and the press is simple – back off.  I take this incident as an attempt by the CID – and the Kenya Government – to obstruct justice at The Hague”.
