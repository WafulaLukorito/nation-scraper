## What you need to know:

* Members expect to fill Cabinet secretary, principal secretary and leadership positions in Parliament in exchange for supporting Jubilee.
* But critics of Mr Musyoka accuse him of not being committed to developing Ukambani, his political bedrock, accusations he has rubbished.

Wiper Democratic Movement's efforts to seek a post-election agreement with Jubilee Party has drawn mixed reactions in Ukambani.

Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka sparked a flurry of reactions a fortnight ago when he announced that his party was in talks with Jubilee and Kanu for a post-election [working relationship](https://www.nation.co.ke/news/politics/Plans-to-forge-a-Uhuru-Raila-coalition-rule/1064-5534730-5p6a5s/index.html) ahead of the 2022 polls.

Some Kamba professionals quickly joined Mr Musyoka’s loyalists in endorsing the move, touted as a way of entrenching national unity and spurring development.

“No community can achieve sustainable development in isolation,” the professionals said last week in a statement signed by, among others, Kenya Medical Training College board chairman Phillip Kaloki and Kenya Film Classification Board boss Ezekiel Mutua.

“Further, there can be no meaningful peace without peaceful engagements with other communities , and therefore, efforts towards alliances and collaboration with other communities and political parties, particularly the ruling party, (are) not only strategic but also wise.”

Mr Musyoka’s supporters are upbeat that the conflict between politicians allied to President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy William Ruto will see Jubilee relinquish several lucrative positions in the government and Parliament for Wiper members to occupy.

“There is no way we can work with Jubilee when Wiper members are not benefiting. Democracy is guided by the principle of scratch my back I scratch yours,” Mavoko MP Patrick Makau said this week when he distributed relief food in the constituency.

**GROWING REBELLION**

Members of the party, he said, expect to fill Cabinet secretary, principal secretary, parastatal and leadership positions in both the National Assembly and the Senate in exchange for supporting Jubilee.

The post-election coalition talks come as Mr Musyoka battles a sustained rebellion from veteran politicians, including former Kitui Senator David Musila, former Machakos Senator Johnson Muthama, and governors Alfred Mutua (Machakos), Charity Ngilu (Kitui) and Kivutha Kibwana (Makueni).

There is also some growing “sibling rivalry” among some of his lieutenants, who are eyeing various seats ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Critics of the Kamba political kingpin accuse him of not being committed to developing Ukambani, his political bedrock, accusations that Mr Musyoka has continually rubbished as unfounded.

Mr Muthama, Mr Musyoka’s latest friend turned foe, leads a team of at least three MPs, mainly from Kitui and Machakos counties, who are openly sympathetic to Dr Ruto. The outspoken politician accuses Mr Musyoka of being indecisive.

The March 2018 Handshake between President Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga prompted Mr Muthama to convene a community meeting to chart a post-National Super Alliance (Nasa) path.

**LIFE AFTER NASA**

On the other hand, some Kamba elders, the region’s key religious leaders and Wiper party leadership called for the Kamba to remain united, and urged Mr Musyoka to lead the community to cross over to President Kenyatta’s side, as [Nasa](https://www.nation.co.ke/dailynation/news/politics/raila-is-bullying-us-nasa-partners-310862) had shown signs of disintegration after it lost to Jubilee in the 2017 polls.

The Wiper leader subsequently made the first move towards that end during his father’s funeral in 2018, when he declared he would henceforth work with the President.

This landed him a plum government [assignment in South Sudan](https://www.nation.co.ke/news/politics/Kalonzo-How-we-brokered-Machar--Kiir-peace-pact/1064-5466602-7awq5/index.html) and saw a handful of his loyal supporters land lucrative government deals. But the tokenism did not end the grumbling in the community, which has over two million votes.

“Like Gideon Moi, he should have shown real political muscle by entering into a legal coalition, not a loose cooperation or partnership. That would mean Jubilee, Wiper and Kanu would be the core of the future broad-based government of national unity,” Prof Kibwana said on Tuesday during the burial of former Makueni MP and Moi-era provincial commissioner Peter Kiilu.

Prof Kibwana is among those who see Wiper’s “belated” move to team up with Jubilee and Kanu as part of a raft of Mr Musyoka’s political miscalculations that put his 2022 State House ambition at stake.

“In 2013, Mr Musyoka missed apex politics. So his 2007 momentum was wasted. He should go back to his primary constituency and ask them what kind and quality of political engagement they want going into 2022,” Prof Kibwana told the *Sunday Nation.*
