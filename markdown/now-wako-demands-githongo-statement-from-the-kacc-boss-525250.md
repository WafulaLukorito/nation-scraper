# Now Wako demands Githongo statement from the Kacc boss

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2006-11-11T21:00:00Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/news/now-wako-demands-githongo-statement-from-the-kacc-boss-525250  

---

Attorney-General Amos Wako has introduced a new demand before starting prosecution of Anglo Leasing cases - he wants anti-corruption boss Aaron Ringera to provide evidence collected from former Ethics and Governance permanent secretary John Githongo over the scandal.

Mr Wako

Mr Wako is reported to have written to Mr Ringera expressing concern that Mr Githongo's evidence was not included when the KACC director presented his office with various files recommending prosecution over the multi-billion-shilling Anglo Leasing scandal.

Apart from other reasons such as "glaring gaps and deficiencies", it is understood Mr Wako wanted to scrutinise what Mr Githongo told the commission alongside other evidence gathered from investigations.

With Mr Githongo's evidence, Mr Wako will be able to know how to approach the prosecution considering that the defence was curiously awaiting the files.

Mr Ringera

However yesterday, sources in the commission said the Githongo dossier will not form part of the evidence because the former PS had not signed his statement.

But Mr Githongo's lawyer, Prof Makau Mutua, told the *Sunday Nation* from the US that his client had not only signed the statements but also sent a comprehensive statement to President Kibaki and Mr Justice Ringera on the matter.

Prof Mutua said: "I am shocked and appalled that Mr Justice Aaron Ringera did not include evidence and testimony provided to him by Mr John Githongo, especially after he spent two days with us in London taking evidence."

The law don said they had cooperated with Mr Justice Ringera in every way he asked them to. In addition, they had provided him with "very detailed, instructive, and impeccable evidence relating" to the Anglo Leasing scandal.

Prof Mutua added: "Among other things, we submitted to him a signed and authenticated statement responding to questions that he had put to us in writing. In short, we have provided him with everything he had asked for."

He said he was baffled that the commission did not deem it fit to consider Mr Githongo's evidence.

"Mr Justice Ringera is a highly learned professor of evidence - in fact he taught me the subject at the Faculty of Law at the University of Nairobi. So, I find it incredulous that he could have done a sloppy and incompetent job."

Mr Githongo

Prof Mutua said the move to shelve Mr Githongo's evidence was meant to cover up the scandal.

"He is involved in an exercise to cover up or sabotage the prosecution of those implicated in the Anglo Leasing scandals. I can only, therefore, conclude that there is more than meets the eye in his conduct."

Nominated MP Mutula Kilonzo said KACC must include the evidence obtained from the former anti-corruption PS.

Mr Kilonzo, a lawyer, said the dossier will form part of the evidence for the defence to destroy the prosecution's case.

He asked: "What will you be telling Kenyans if you leave the Githongo dossier out?"

The Kanu MP said it was by virtue of the dossier that the country learnt of the magnitude of the scandal.

The tapes he released, he added, forced former Justice minister Kiraitu Murungi to resign.

And it was pressure from Mr Githongo's revelations that made Finance minister David Mwiraria to resign as well. The two ministers have said they were clean and had nothing to do with the scandal.

However, Mr Kilonzo argued that Mr Wako wanted the evidence from the former PS so that he could destroy it before going to court.

Mr Kilonzo said: "Mr Wako cannot go to court unless he has discounted the Githongo dossier. And the only way to discount it is to have it on the files."

Law Society of Kenya council member Evans Monari argued that although it was important to have the evidence from Mr Githongo, it was not the only one available.

Parliament, through the Public Accounts Committee, he said, had gathered evidence as well.

"Githongo's evidence is good evidence but it is not the only available, there is evidence that can allow the prosecutor to take a case to court and successfully prosecute it," Mr Monari added.

At the same time, Mr Monari dismissed KACC's argument that it cannot present Mr Githongo's statement as evidence because it had not been signed. According to him, the courts can decide to verify the evidence from Mr Githongo himself through a commission in London. This allows evidence to be given on oath to an authorised officer in London - judge or magistrate - and it will be accepted in Kenyan courts.

"Mr Githongo's signing or non-signing of the statement is immaterial. His evidence can be verified through a commission," he said.

It was Justice minister Martha Karua who said that the former PS had not signed the statement he issued to KACC in London and called on him to return home to sign it to speed up investigations into the questionable security contracts. But Mr Githongo dismissed Ms Karua, saying she was "flatly wrong" and accused her of engaging in distractions and misinformation.

"For the record, let me categorically state that Ms Karua is flatly wrong and I can only speculate on the basis of her intervention on behalf of KACC, which is an autonomous commission, on how and why she would be privy to its investigations," he said.

Ms Karua had asked Mr Githongo to come back to Kenya and sign his statement to enable the Kenya Anti-Corruption Commission speed up investigations into questionable security contracts.
