# Lawsuits seeking to abolish Ruto sea of advisers now 3

**Date:** 2025-06-03T17:09:07Z

A rights group has petitioned a Nairobi court to disband the President's Council of Economic Advisers headed by economist David Ndii in order to invalidate the establishment of a “kitchen Cabinet” in the Executive.

Katiba Institute wants the High Court to revoke President William Ruto's decision to hire and establish posts in the Executive to [reward political loyalists.](https://nation.africa/kenya/news/taxpayers-to-foot-sh1-1bn-bill-for-ruto-s-top-advisers--4618620)

The lobby argues that the growing list of advisers has ballooned the public wage bill at a time the government is struggling to finance public debt, adding that the council ought to cut its spending.

According to court papers, the lobby wants the High Court to declare that creation of adviser offices and subsequent appointments to the posts are unconstitutional.

Katiba Institute accuses President Ruto of usurping the powers of constitutional commissions, leading to duplication of roles and imprudent use of public resources.

The lawsuit is targeting the abolishment of 13 advisory offices established by the President and filled with political loyalists numbering at least 21.

The offices listed in the petition include:

1. President’s Council of Economic Advisers
2. Office of Food Security and Animal Production Adviser
3. Government Delivery Services
4. Office of the National Security Adviser
5. Office of the Women’s Rights Adviser
6. Office of the Council of Climate Change Adviser
7. Office of Fiscal Affairs and Budget Policy
8. Office of the Economic Transformation
9. Adviser on Livestock and Rangeland Management
10. Senior Adviser of Constitutional Affairs
11. Senior Political Adviser
12. Special Adviser on youth economic empowerment
13. Office of the Senior Economic Advisee

## Job beneficiaries

Among the prominent beneficiaries of the contested offices is Dr David Ndii, social media strategist Dennis Itumbi, former Prime Minister Raila Odinga's nephew Jaoko Oburu, Prof Makau Mutua, former Solicitor-General Kennedy Ogeto and lawyer Harriet Chiggai among others.

Dr David Ndii, the Chairperson of Presidential Council of Economic Advisers.

Photo credit: Billy Ogada | Nation Media Group

The petitioners contend that the hiring has led to a bloated Executive, which Kenyans had corrected in 2010 through the new Constitution by capping the number of Cabinet Secretaries and Principal Secretaries “who are supposed to be technocrats and should be the ones to advise the President”.

“Worryingly, these offices were neither created with the recommendation or consultation of the Public Service Commission (PSC) and the Salaries and Remuneration (SRC) as required by the Constitution, nor were they created after satisfying the conditions laid out in the Public Service Commission Act. Further, the appointment of people to fill the unconstitutional offices was made contrary to the requirements of Article 232 of the Constitution,” argues the organisation’s lawyer Malidzo Nyawa in the petition.

“The ugly head of prebendalism and the use of public appointments to reward political loyalties or keep cronies in public service, at the expense of equally qualified individuals, is yet again rearing its head. Contrary to the express provisions of the Constitution, the President has unilaterally handpicked a number of persons and appointed them to various offices, nicknamed as the advisors to the president,” says Mr Nyawa.

The petition brings the number of cases challenging the hiring of presidential advisers to three.

The two previous cases were filed by [activist Eliud Matindi](https://nation.africa/kenya/news/politics/why-makau-mutua-s-job-as-ruto-advisor-is-at-risk--5041080) and [lawyer Suyianka Lempaa](https://nation.africa/kenya/news/ruto-advisers-sued-to-refund-millions-for-illegal-offices--5058664). All are pending hearing and determination.
