# New Constitution is our ticket out of self-ignorance

## What you need to know:

* In my view, the laws of a country are its brain. All the knowledge of a country is contained in its laws

May be I should stick to law and politics because that’s my training. But I can’t resist opining on my Four-Square Brain theory. I am not a psychologist, but neither am I a quack. But, for over two decades, I have been vexed by my Four-Square Brain theory.

I don’t know how I stumbled upon it, but one day — long ago — I had a eureka moment. From a crude idea, I have since refined the Four-Square Brain theory to make it applicable to Kenya.

My theory is an anatomical concept about “knowing” and “self-knowledge”. It’s what separates “dumb” societies from “intelligent” ones. Like human beings, no state or society can prosper unless it understands the “four squares” of its brain.

In my theory, I imagine that the brain is a “square box” divided unequally into four smaller squares. As we know, the brain is the “nerve centre” of the nervous system in all animals, including human beings.

The brain controls the entire body and all its functions — no organs can work, and no movement or thought is possible, without the brain.

**Irreversible end**

No sensation or feeling is possible without the brain. The brain centralises in itself all the functions in a human body. A “brain death” indicates the “irreversible end” of all brain activity and is normally enough to sustain a ruling of “legal death”. One is dead if their brain is dead. Nothing matters more than the brain.

If the above is true of the human brain — and it is — where is the Kenyan brain? In my view, the Constitution of a country is its brain. All the knowledge of a country is contained in the Constitution.

“Stupid” or “dysfunctional” countries have “unintelligent” or “unworkable” constitutions. Take, for example, Kenya’s previous Constitution. That document was the product of an “imperfect birth”. It was a “deformed” Constitution that became an “abusive” and “tyrannical” adult.

Its brain was warped and underdeveloped. We spent decades trying to get rid of it and — by the sheer dint of persistence — we prevailed in August last year. But what kind of Constitution did we give “birth” to? Is it intelligent and loving?

In my theory, the brain has four unequal squares. Each square is a chamber of “knowledge”. It’s either “self-knowledge” or “knowledge by others”. Think of this as “Mutua’s Brain”. Square A of Mutua’s Brain contains only that which Mutua alone knows.

**The external world**

No one else but Mutua knows what is in Square A. Square B of Mutua’s Brain contains only what others — the external world — know about Mutua.

Mutua knows nothing in Square B. Square C of Mutua’s Brian contains knowledge held “in common” by Mutua and the external world.

Everybody knows what is in Square C. Square D of Mutua’s Brain is a “black box”. No one — not even Mutua or the external world — knows what’s in Square D.

In my theory, Square A is the largest chamber in the most “intelligent” human beings. This gives the individual the most “self-awareness” which is the source of intelligence.

This person is able to maximise his strengths and minimise his weaknesses. For him, squares “B” and “C” are much smaller because they’ve been “eaten up” by “A”.

Significantly, Square D — the zone of “ignorance,” “stupidity,” “unawareness,” and “lack of self-knowledge” — has been shrunk in this individual. Square D is largest in the least developed people.

Squares “B” and “C” are common in village wags and many public figures, especially politicians. The key to the highest form of human intelligence is to shrink much of “D” — the “black box” — into “A”. My theory begs several questions.

Where does Kenya’s new Constitution fit in this? What are its four squares? What’s in those four squares? How large are they?

I believe that Square A — the Constitution’s self-awareness of the state and country is impeccable.

That’s why its central organising principle is an open government that’s accountable to the people. Opacity in every institution of the state — including armed, spy, and security agencies — is a thing of the past. Public participation is the new norm.

The Executive is still the first among equals, but is stripped of rule by fiat and subjected to oversight by the Legislature and kept within the law by the Judiciary.

The “margins” are now at the “centre”. The Constitution’s black box — Square D — has been shrunk considerably. While there’s still much we still don’t know, very few things have been left to chance.

But this is as it should be; so that we can grow by filling in the blanks. For example, we will have to work out the relationship between the “upper” and the “lower” houses of Parliament. The Senate, an unknown creature in modern Kenya, will have to develop an identity.

**Less hallowed status**

The National Assembly will have to adjust to a less hallowed status. County governors will have to learn to practise democracy at the “grassroots”.

Political parties will have to be real. The courts will become fountains of justice. This is uncharted territory.

Above all, Kenyans will have to become “new citizens”. The days of “kitu kidogo” or petty bribery will be gone.

So will official “grand larceny” of the public purse. “Tribal racism” will be uncool and illegal. Every Kenyan will become a “vigilante for justice”.

We know that in human history no society has ever become great without great citizens and visionary leaders. In my Four-Square Brain theory lies the genius for higher human intelligence.

The new Constitution is the “potion” that we must drink to get there. It’s our ticket out of self-ignorance.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
