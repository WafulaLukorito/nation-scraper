# Why the PDM is political gimmickry 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2010-03-13T14:51:49Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/why-the-pdm-is-political-gimmickry--626886  

---

One constant in Kenyan politics is the underestimation of the hoi polloi by the political class. The reason for this political chicanery is that it works virtually every time on the hapless public.

All you have to do is watch the political rallies every weekend. The least advantaged Kenyans throng the rallies only to be fed empty and vacuous rhetoric by the elite.

That’s why the PNU can take the licence to christen itself the Progressive Democratic Movement without giving a single thought to any substantive distinction between the two vessels beyond the change of name.

**Intellectual laziness**

Is this intellectual laziness on the part of the political elite, or simply disrespect for Kenyans? I have concluded that it’s both given the history and anatomy of the political landscape.

The PNU leaders know that Kenyans don’t really care about the ideology or policy platform of a party. Heck, Kenyans don’t even care where the façade is a party or not.

The bad news is that politicians know that Kenyans don’t care about these important indices of political life. You don’t need to be a nuclear scientist to know that all most Kenyans care about is the tribal identity of the leaders of our so-called political parties.

It’s all about the tribe in Kenya. Nothing else really matters. Not even water, food, education, or security.

That’s why Bethuel Kiplagat, the belligerent chair of the discredited truth commission, can argue that he’s being hounded out of office because of his ethnic identity.

It is the same reason the so-called Kipsigis Council of Elders feels empowered to defend him on a purely tribal basis. Kenyans have no shame when it comes to playing the tribal card.

This is the first card they see when they wake up, and the last card they see when they go to sleep. This is the tragedy of our country.

I figure this is PNU’s logic. Change the name to PDM — or whatever — to hoodwink the people that it’s a new and dynamic party, but retain the same cast of characters at the centre. That’s the key – the same “characters at the centre’’.

There is no surprise here because PDM revolves around Kalonzo Musyoka, Uhuru Kenyatta, George Saitoti and Kiraitu Murungi.

Soon – very soon – you will be able to throw in William Ruto and Najib Balala. Could there be a more tired cast of characters than these? What’s shocking is that they think they can fool you by wearing a shirt with a different colour!

What is PDM exactly? Is it “progressive” as its progenitors claim? Do its leaders know what “progressive” means in political language? Is it “democratic” as they claim? Perhaps, most curiously, is it a “movement” as we understand the word in social and political theory?

**Call a spoon a shovel**

I would have expected Mr Murungi, who has intellectual ambitions, to hide his cynicism. Why would serious people call a spoon a shovel unless they were utterly cynical and knew that no one else knew the difference? PDM is neither progressive nor democratic. Nor is it even a movement.

PDM is fiction – pure and simple. It’s a figment of the imagination by idle political minds that have contempt for the Kenyan people.

They think you can put lipstick on a pig and trick people about its biology. But why are Kenyans willing dupes in this game of political nonsense?

Why do we act as though we are politically retarded? I have an answer. It’s the tyranny of the tribe. We are imprisoned in the cave of the tribe. Mr Musyoka and Mr Kenyatta know this very well, as do their fellow PDM travellers.

But they also know one more thing – we will not look beyond their tribal identities to unmask PDM. In fact, we don’t care whether PDM stands for anything as long as Mr Musyoka and Mr Kenyatta are at the helm!

It’s often said that a country gets the leaders it deserves. I don’t quite agree with this harsh statement although I think there is some truth to it.

Look at Nazi Germany. There is little doubt that Adolf Hitler – the monster – was wildly popular. That’s why the pogroms of the Nazis and the Holocaust were not just Hitler’s doing alone.

The same is true of the genocide in Rwanda. It was not just the leaders who were culpable. The common folk participated in these abominations to make them possible. That’s why “nations” – and individuals – must be adjudged guilty for such heinous atrocities.

Only Kenyans can free themselves from the killer disease called tribalism. Remember it’s not in the interest of the political class to cure this illness.

The political class thrives – and prospers – from the malignancy of tribalism. Point a finger at a crook and he will say that you are “finishing” his tribe.  
I wish we could “finish” all the tribes by “nationalising” them – transforming every horrid tribesman into a Kenyan national.

We have no chance if we don’t kill the tribe. This is the reason we must reject gimmicks like PDM which are tribal masquerades.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
