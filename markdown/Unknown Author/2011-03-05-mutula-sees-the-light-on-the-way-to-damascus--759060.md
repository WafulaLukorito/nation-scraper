# Mutula ‘sees the light on the way to Damascus’ 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2011-03-05T15:32:09Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/mutula-sees-the-light-on-the-way-to-damascus--759060  

---

## What you need to know:

* He senses that Kenyans are changing, but the leaders are not. He does not want to be a political dinosaur like them

Justice minister Mutula Kilonzo has been acting as though he has “heard the good news” and “seen the light”. Like the biblical Saul, the zealous Pharisee who intensely persecuted the followers of Jesus, Mr Kilonzo is imitating Paul.

In the Acts of the Apostles, Saul often took the road from Jerusalem to Damascus to arrest the followers of Jesus. He usually returned the captives to Jerusalem for torture and execution.

But, “on his day of salvation,” the evil sojourn is interrupted on the Damascus road. A blinding light hits him, and he communicates with a divine voice. And this act marks the genesis of the “conversion” of Saul to Paul.

I know Mr Kilonzo is no Paul, but has he become the conscience of the PNU/ODM coalition government? The Mbooni MP has had a legendary legal career. A legal eagle, Mr Kilonzo was the brightest of Kanu lawyers under former President Daniel arap Moi.

He was, in fact, Mr Moi’s personal lawyer. A blue-eyed boy of the Moi era, Mr Kilonzo was a sworn defender of the single party Kanu kleptocracy. He never met a reformer or human rights activist he did not despise.

But he did very well for himself. No numbers are available, but you can bet he’s a very wealthy man.

A poor child from rural Machakos, Mr Kilonzo was the first student in East Africa to get a First Class law degree from the University of Dar es Salaam. A Senior Counsel, he has genius-like smarts.

Within the Cabinet – and Parliament – Mr Kilonzo has become a breath of fresh air. While both ODM and PNU MPs sink the country into despair, Mr Kilonzo has stood tall. He’s a rare voice of common sense and reason.

Tranquil and statesman-like, Mr Kilonzo is evolving into a national leader. He appears to be guided by nothing else but simple morality and the national interest.

In my study of Kenyan politics, I haven’t come across such a remarkable transformation of a senior politician this late in life. Now in his early 60s, Mr Kilonzo is quietly turning the “Saul” in him to “Paul”. Never say never – we can all be redeemed.

I have four incontrovertible examples of Mr Kilonzo’s miraculous mutation on the road to Damascus. The first is his defiance of the ethnic myopia of ODM-K, PNU’s key ally. Mr Kilonzo has refused to sing from the same tribal hymnbook as VP Kalonzo Musyoka, his party leader.

**Herd mentality**

He won’t go along with the herd mentality of Kamba ODM-K MPs. He’s made it clear that his conscience and convictions aren’t for sale.

His most important stand has been against the PNU/KKK cabal. He has opposed virtually every nefarious plot by the PNU/KKK syndicate.

Unlike most ODM and PNU MPs, Mr Kilonzo has rejected sabre-rattling, tribal-caucusing, and ethnic-baiting.

Secondly, Mr Kilonzo has vocally opposed the PNU/KKK plot to sabotage The Hague trials of the Ocampo Six. He publicly stood against the scheme to pull Kenya out of the Rome Statute of the ICC.

He has been scathing in his attack of VP Musyoka’s “shuttle diplomacy” to lobby the AU and the UN Security Council for a deferral of the ICC cases.

He warned that attempts to sabotage The Hague trials can only isolate Kenya internationally. He’s a rare Cabinet member with enough guts to openly call for the Ocampo Six to face justice at The Hague. He’s done so at great peril to his Cabinet post.

Thirdly, Mr Kilonzo famously opposed President Mwai Kibaki’s nominations of candidates for the positions of CJ, AG, DPP, and Director of Budget. His defiance – with others – over the illegal nominations forced Mr Kibaki to retreat.

But Mr Kilonzo went even further and argued that the illegality of the nominations could not have been cured by consultations between President Kibaki and PM Raila Odinga. To him – and this is the law – all these posts must be advertised for competitive searches.

In this, Mr Kilonzo is the democratic conscience of the government. Who would have thought this former Kanu hawk would risk his career to fight for the letter and spirit of the new Constitution?

Lastly, Mr Kilonzo has transformed the ministry of Justice from being a “poodle” of State House.

Under minister Kiraitu Murungi, the ministry was an anvil for Mr Kibaki to use as he pleased. That’s how Bomas was sabotaged. It’s how the Anglo Leasing investigations were botched and covered up.

As minister, Gichugu MP Martha Karua did better than Mr Murungi, but had to quit when President Kibaki sidelined her. She became “irrelevant” and enemy of State House when she wouldn’t do Mr Kibaki’s bidding.

**Kibaki’s bidding**

In contrast, Mr Kilonzo will neither do Mr Kibaki’s bidding, nor resign. He’s double-trouble to the PNU/KKK plotters, but they are powerless to sack him. We are watching to see if they dare.

So why is Mr Kilonzo turning into Paul? I believe it has to do with his native smarts. He knows that the coalition government is suffering from the “Mubarak syndrome”.

This is the ossification of the kleptocracy. It’s the contempt of the people by those in power. But Mr Kilonzo can see – unlike his political classmates – that Kenyans are sick and tired of leaders acting with impunity.

He senses that Kenyans are changing, but their leaders are not. He doesn’t want to be a political dinosaur like them. That’s why he’s becoming Paul. I hope he doesn’t revert to Saul.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
