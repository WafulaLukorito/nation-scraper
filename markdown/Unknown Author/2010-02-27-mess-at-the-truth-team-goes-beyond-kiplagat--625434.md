# Mess at the Truth team goes beyond Kiplagat 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2010-02-27T15:31:30Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/mess-at-the-truth-team-goes-beyond-kiplagat--625434  

---

The resignation of Ambassador Bethuel Kiplagat from the chair of the TJRC cannot, by itself, save the truth commission. The controversy over Mr Kiplagat’s suitability has exposed deep schisms among the commissioners and completely sapped all public credibility from the TJRC.

This particular truth commission is now such a total wreck that it is utterly unable, as currently composed, to recover any public confidence. It is hallucinatory to think that Mr Kiplagat’s departure can right the ship.

The commission has haemorrhaged from the beginning, and is now on its deathbed because of the fratricidal conflicts among the commissioners. It is a house hopelessly divided against itself.

There is no doubt that the appointment of Mr Kiplagat as chair was initially the commission’s death knell. His implication in some of the country’s darkest moments should have automatically disqualified him.

The mention of his name in the same breath with the Wagalla Massacre, the Ouko assassination, Renamo, and the extradition of political exiles places ominously dark clouds over his head.

To make matters worse, the Ndung’u Report names Mr Kiplagat as the alleged beneficiary of illegally allocated parcels of land. It is an open and shut case that Mr Kiplagat smears the truth commission with his past and is simply unfit to lead it.

Even though Mr Kiplagat has been in denial, the letters he has produced ironically prove his culpability! Why is he so stubbornly clinging to a job for which the public wants him out? Has he been “truthful” about his past? Has he done “justice” to all the questions that have been raised about him? How can he “reconcile” himself to the serious charges levelled against him?

The chair of a truth commission must be above suspicion and beyond reproach. I have heard it said that Mr Kiplagat thinks that his critics are driven by ethnic motives. Unfortunately, this is the bogeyman of Kenyan politics.

Impunity reigns supreme because officials in Kenya usually escape accountability by seeking cover behind the tribe. If true, this is a sad cry of desperation by Mr Kiplagat.

But the ills that torment the TJRC go beyond Mr Kiplagat. The chair’s tribulations have exposed the vanity and vacuousness of most of the commissioners.

Their positions and actions should disqualify them from sitting on the commission.

First, those commissioners who supported Mr Kiplagat – and defended him against insurmountable evidence – must quit. How can they be expected to discharge the somber obligation of their office if they defended a tainted chair?

This may mean that they are likely to shield other wrongdoers who appear before the commission. It also shows that they are more concerned with burying the uncomfortable truth than seeking accountability.

How can the public trust commissioners who lack the courage and integrity to stand up for the truth?

Second, it seems that most of the commissioners see the TJRC as a lucrative job, and not a public service.

There have been suggestions that some commissioners cannot contemplate leaving the TJRC because of the fat paycheques. This is wrongheaded. It makes a mockery of the commission. This is why Kenyans are fed up with commissions that milk the public only to produce nothing.

One would have thought that truth commissioners hearken to higher calling, and are not driven by the crass materialism that seems to define Kenyan elites.

The TJRC is not just another feeding trough where scarce public funds are siphoned off. External donors must stop funding the TJRC until it is reconstituted as a credible body.

Third, no truth commission can effectively do its job if the commissioners are at war with each other. The fight over the chairman’s fate has left the TJRC hopelessly divided and paralysed. The commissioners do not trust or respect each other.

How can the TJRC be expected to function when bad blood now runs so deep? How can commissioners who are at each others’ throats – and who have accused each other of tribalism and malevolence – conduct hearings on the most sensitive abominations in Kenya’s history with integrity?

If they could not handle the Kiplagat issue, how will they fare when bigger fish appear before them accused of the most heinous violations? Kenyans are right to wonder whether some of the commissioners have a price.

Last, I disagree with those who say that if this TJRC is disbanded Kenya will never get another truth commission. Such historical determinism is silly. No one knows what the future holds.

What I know is that Kenyans will not accept a sham truth commission. Rather than ram down the country’s throat a deformed and discredited institution, we should go back to the drawing board.

Immediately disband the TJRC. Then rewrite the truth commission law and repeal any other contradictory legislation.

Crucially, commissioners to a new TJRC should be headhunted, not asked to apply. This will make sure that only the most morally credible and qualified individuals become commissioners.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
