* The letter was supposedly written by members of the outlawed Mungiki sect and warned Judges not to rule against Jubilee Presidential candidate Uhuru Kenyatta and his running mate William Ruto.

Police are pursuing two motives regarding a threatening letter sent to Chief Justice Willy Mutunga.

An investigation report seen by the Sunday Nation says the letter was drafted either to achieve a political agenda or to oppose judicial reforms.

“Enquiries conducted point out that there are some discontent among judicial staff regarding the ongoing reforms by the CJ. It’s also believed that a political agenda is involved,” reads part of the report.

Detectives followed the letter trail before it landed at Dr Mutunga’s desk but did not succeed in identifying its author.

“The envelope was taken to the document examination unit of the CID to subject it to magnification of the stamp impression. The investigation team sought the help of the cyber crimes unit to locate the author via the Internet. It emerged that the threat letter was sent to 149 people through Twitter by one Mr Makau Mutua,” the report said.

Prof Mutua is Dean and Distinguished Professor at Suny Buffalo Law School and Chair of the Kenya Human Rights Commission.

Police did not say whether they interviewed Prof Mutua.

“The contents of the letter received through the CJ office and the contents of the letter downloaded from the Twitter account are the same. However the footer of the letter from the CJ is typed whilst the one downloaded appears handwritten,” the police report says.

The letter was supposedly written by members of the outlawed Mungiki sect and warned Judges not to rule against Jubilee Presidential candidate Uhuru Kenyatta and his running mate William Ruto.
