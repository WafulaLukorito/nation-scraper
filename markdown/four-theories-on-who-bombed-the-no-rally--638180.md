# Four theories on who bombed the ‘No’ rally 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2010-06-19T16:17:16Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/four-theories-on-who-bombed-the-no-rally--638180  

---

I have four theories – one of them a conspiracy – on who bombed the ‘No’ campaign rally at Uhuru Park, and why. The illegal edit of the draft constitution was the initial act of sabotage.

Then came the bizarre ruling declaring kadhi courts “illegal’’. Now the terrorist attack and murder and injury of innocents at Uhuru park on a sabbath. Evildoers are stalking Kenya. Very powerful and dark forces are afraid – very afraid – of the draft constitution. There is a demonic cabal of anti-reformers who would rather drive Kenya to a civil war than accept the new constitution.

This axis of evil will do anything – legal and illegal – to push Kenya off the cliff before the referendum. The pattern of sabotage points to the culprits. The illegal edit was supposed to make the constitution “illegal” and stop the referendum. The ruling on kadhi courts was meant to ignite an inferno of opposition. The killings at Uhuru park – a traitor’s act that borders on treason – was meant to provoke civil conflict.

Each act of sabotage has been more desperate than the last. Anti-reformers have upped the ante with each successive act. I don’t think it’s an accident that after every act of sabotage, the ‘No’ leaders demand a postponement of the referendum. Do they know something we don’t?

I have three real theories for those culpable. First, I suspect the “watermelons” for engineering the Uhuru park attack. The “watermelons” – green on the outside and red on the inside – are PNU leaders who purport to support the constitution but don’t. This is the classic definition of a saboteur. Saboteurs are cowards who have no guts to openly stand up for their convictions. My “watermelon theory” is that PNU politicos see the draft constitution as Prime Minister Raila Odinga’s vehicle to State House.

The “watermelons” had hoped to succeed President Kibaki. They stand to gain if the referendum is stopped. Secondly, I suspect those likely to be indicted by Luis Moreno-Ocampo and the International Criminal Court. Unless you live on Mars, it’s clear that the noose is tightening on the masterminds of the 2008 post-election violence.

American Vice-President Joe Biden made it clear last week that the Kenya Government has no choice but to hand over those indicted to the ICC. President Barack Obama himself has left no doubt the guillotine awaits the masterminds. There is nowhere to hide. Every indication is that Mr Kibaki and Mr Odinga will hand over the suspects to The Hague.

The suspects can only delay their date with justice if they plunge the country into a civil war. This would be a solid motive for the Uhuru Park attack. Thirdly, I suspect some leaders in the ‘No’ camp for staging the attack on their own supporters and blaming it on their opponents. This is very cynical, but it would not be the first time one attacked their own to make the enemy look bad and seek public sympathy.

It happens with an alarming frequency in Iraq. It’s a tactical military strategy that could be a game changer. Sacrifice a few troops for victory. By blaming the attack on their opponents, the ‘No’ side portrays the ‘Yes’ camp as bent on victory by any means necessary, including murder.

They have accused the State – which is Mr Kibaki and Mr Odinga, the leaders of the ‘Yes’ camp – of gagging, persecuting and killing ‘No’ supporters. The stakes don’t get any higher than this. Let me now dispense with one conspiracy theory. A conspiracy theory is not a real theory, but a fringe hypothesis that’s rooted in paranoia.

This is the perfect description for those who would blame the Uhuru Park attack on Muslims.It’s a cockamamie yarn that’s spun by hateful bigots. Muslims – like all genuine ‘Yes’ supporters – have no reason to murder ‘No’ proponents. The simple reason is that their side is winning big.

It’s counter-intuitive for a player on a winning squad to commit a deliberately flagrant foul against the losing team in the fourth quarter. Why would he give the other team an unearned chance to score points? It’s like scoring in one’s goal. Scapegoating Muslims has no merit. Those peddling this ridiculous conspiracy theory must cease forthwith.

The attack could have been executed by renegade security officers working with the “watermelons” or the ‘No’ leaders. The timing of the attack was suspicious. It took place after most of the leaders had left the rally.

Did the attackers intend to “sacrifice” the masses and spare the leaders? This twist adds suspicion to the ‘No’ camp leaders and raises questions about what they knew, when they knew it, and what they did. As the referendum campaign enters the final stretch, Kenya’s security forces must remain on high alert. The intelligence community must keep its ear to the ground.

The country cannot afford another attack, or a retaliatory counterattack. We cannot grant anti-reformers their wish. The referendum cannot be postponed – Kenyans will speak at the ballot box in August.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC*
