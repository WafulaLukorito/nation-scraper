# Kibaki nominees are fruits of a poisoned tree 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2011-02-04T18:44:37Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/kibaki-nominees-are-fruits-of-a-poisoned-tree--755500  

---

President Mwai Kibaki is really an enigma in Kenyan politics. He “rules” without “governing”.

Nothing illustrates this more than Mr Kibaki’s nominations of new judicial officers – Justice Alnashir Visram for Chief Justice, Professor Githu Muigai for Attorney-General, and lawyer Kioko Kilukumi for Director of Public Prosecutions.

With an air of imperium, Mr Kibaki sprung the “appointments” on Kenyans as though they were “routine” and “uncontroversial”. Nothing could be further from the truth.

Since Kenya’s key problem is impunity, no other constitutional offices are more important. Which begs the question – why would Mr Kibaki act unilaterally? Who really is calling the shots at State House?

Both the High Court and Prime Minister Raila Odinga have rightly rejected the nominations as “illegal” and “unconstitutional”. So have the Judicial Service Commission and the Commission on the Implementation of the Constitution.

Key sectors of society – LSK, the Catholic Church, FIDA, KHRC, and the ICJ – have termed President Kibaki’s power grab an act of impunity.

But, in a political dodge, Speaker Kenneth Marende wimped out and refused to call a spade by its name. The spirit and black letter law of the National Accord and the Constitution prohibit nominations by “presidential decree”.

President Kibaki needs to comprehend that the Constitution isn’t a worthless piece of paper. He should fire his devious and incompetent legal and political advisers pronto.

I believe there are two main reasons Mr Kibaki attempted to jam these nominations down the throats of Kenyans.

The first, and most important, reason for the nominations is the high plot to save the Ocampo Six from the International Criminal Court.

This may not be Mr Kibaki’s doing per se, but I believe that he’s become captive to the PNU/KKK cabal. VP Kalonzo Musyoka and Finance minister Uhuru Kenyatta – the two favoured PNU/KKK presidential candidates – may have taken Mr Kibaki hostage.

Add Eldoret North MP Willian Ruto to the mix and you get the picture. The other key movers in this cloak-and-dagger subterfuge are Energy minister Kiraitu Murungi and Internal Security minister George Saitoti.

But the true mastermind – the key intriguer at the inner sanctum of power – is Head of Civil Service Ambassador Francis Muthaura. He has a personal stake in scuttling The Hague trials of the Ocampo Six because he is one of their number.

Mr Muthaura is “indispensable” to Mr Kibaki’s regime. I think Mr Kibaki believes that he can neither rule nor govern without Mr Muthaura.

Mr Muthaura and the cabal of Messrs Kenyatta, Musyoka, Murungi, Saitoti – and now Ruto – have convinced Mr Kibaki that he needs them for his political legacy and survival.

The PNU/KKK alliance is behind VP Musyoka’s “shuttle diplomacy” to lobby the AU and the UN to kill The Hague trials of the Ocampo Six.

The PNU/KKK cabal believes that Kenya’s case for a deferral of The Hague trials hinges on the establishment of a credible local judicial process.

That’s why Mr Kibaki’s nominees for CJ, AG, and DPP were meant to hoodwink the international community.

That is, convince the UN and the ICC that Kenya is “able and willing” to bring the perpetrators of post-election violence to book.

But I am afraid the nominations – which are unilateral, unconstitutional, toxic, and politically unacceptable – have had the opposite effect.

The nominations are proof positive to the UN and the ICC that Mr Kibaki wants to protect the Ocampo Six by installing judicial officers perceived to be partial to the PNU/KKK alliance.

The second reason for the unilateral nominations is the PNU/KKK plot to alienate Mr Odinga from Mr Kibaki and block his path to State House in 2012.

The PNU/KKK syndicate has worked hard to break the bond between Mr Odinga and Mr Ruto and the Kalenjin. That chasm is now irrevocable. The PNU/KKK alliance is consolidating.

Mr Kenyatta, Mr Musyoka and Mr Ruto have recovered from the drubbing Mr Odinga inflicted on them in the fight for the new Constitution.

They have put asunder the camaraderie that Mr Odinga built with Mr Kibaki. They have moved in for the kill, and want a permanent divorce between Mr Kibaki and Mr Odinga.

The PNU/KKK alliance knows that Mr Odinga stands to benefit should Mr Ruto and Mr Kenyatta be carted off to The Hague. They believe that Mr Odinga cannot capture the presidency if the PNU/KKK alliance holds.

But they know that the alliance will likely fall apart if Mr Ruto and Mr Kenyatta are sequestered at The Hague. Mr Musyoka doesn’t think he can withstand the Odinga juggernaut by himself.

That’s why he is working overtime to save his PNU/KKK brethren from The Hague. But he miscalculated. He and the PNU/KKK brigade should have “duped” Mr Odinga into rubberstamping Mr Kibaki’s nominees.

The rejection of the nominees by Mr Odinga and the High Court shatters Kenya’s international campaign for a deferral of The Hague trials.

I don’t want to get into the debate about whether President Kibaki’s nominees are qualified for the posts. But I am well aware of the objections raised against them by large sections of society.

What’s important now is to concentrate on the constitutional process by which such appointments must be made “as by law established”.

Mr Kibaki must rescind the nominations because he acted in violation of the Constitution. And Justice Visram, Prof Muigai and Mr Kilukumi must stand down.

They are fruits of a poisoned tree and beneficiaries of impunity. They will never be legitimate if they are perceived as PNU/KKK “lapdogs”.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
