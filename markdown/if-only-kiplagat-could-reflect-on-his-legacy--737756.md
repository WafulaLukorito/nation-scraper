## What you need to know:

* After being spurned by credible sections of civil society, he decided to ‘hire’ victims as TJRC workers

Ambassador Bethuel Kiplagat, the beleaguered chair of the Truth, Justice, and Reconciliation Commission, is a man in the twilight of his career. That’s why he should reflect on his legacy.

How will the epitaph on his tombstone read when he’s done on this earth? It will either read “career diplomat, peacemaker, human rights defender” or “master illusionist, tone-deaf, disgraced chair of the discredited TJRC”.

We are all mere mortals. That’s why we must contemplate our own demise. Will history be kind to the self-professed “ambassador of peace”?

I have not relished the image of “grandpa” being dragged through the mud, but I am not sympathetic to his bull-headedness. He has done it to himself.

I have watched with bemused interest the quixotic attempts by Mr Kiplagat to “breathe life” into the “dead” TJRC. They are doing so without shame, and any regard for the public.

Mr Kiplagat would do well to take a lesson in Japanese political culture. In the “Land of the Rising Sun,” disgraced public officials either resign or commit ritual suicide. I am not suggesting the latter option for Mr Kiplagat, but the man is “politically done”.

The concept of “public shame” demands that he quits rather than defile forever the notion of a truth commission for Kenya. Gluing himself to the chair will not bestow legitimacy on him. He must go home – now.

Nor will dirty tricks help him. Mr Kiplagat reached a new low when he used public funds to soften his critics and “buy off” opportunists. After being spurned by the most credible sections of civil society, Mr Kiplagat decided to “hire” victims as TJRC workers.

I believe the word cynical was invented to describe such a subterfuge. A veteran of the repressive ancient regime, Mr Kiplagat pulled a classic Moi-era masterstroke.

If you can’t silence your opponents, throw them a few crumbs. And the hapless victims scrambled for the scraps from the master’s table. This tells me that Mr Kiplagat is determined to use any means necessary to cling on to the chair.

Apparently, Mr Kiplagat knows very little about the instrument of the truth commission. The most important attribute of a truth commission is its “independence” from victims and perpetrators alike. That’s why it cannot be controlled by suspicious people or staffed by victims. Both antagonists would “inherently” soil it.

Mr Kiplagat does not understand – nor is he capable of accepting the fact – that credibility is the biggest public asset for a truth commission. Public perception is everything. Mr Kiplagat ought to know that his actions run counter to the spirit of the new Constitution.

As a true relic of the past, he is an anachronism who just doesn’t get it. He doesn’t care even if he’s a choir of one.

When all was lost, Betty Kaari Murungi, the TJRC’s moral and legal anchor, quit her posts as vice-chair and, eventually, commissioner. She did so only after working extremely hard within the TJRC to salvage it. She would not continue under Mr Kiplagat, an alleged perpetrator, as chair.

The proper thing for the other commissioners to do – and it’s not too late – is to walk out and let Mr Kiplagat conduct his disgraceful charade by lonesome self. If not, history will judge them harshly – very harshly. They must be reminded that Ms Murungi was the most credible among them. Without her, there is zero credibility at the commission.

But Mr Kiplagat is the master illusionist. Listen to him and you would think that the truth commission is a brand new engine clicking on all cylinders. We are told of “work plans,” “statement-taking exercises,” and “peace and reconciliation forums”.

What a joke! No one – and I mean not a single soul – at the TJRC has the capacity to imagine and carry out such work. Even more importantly, the large majority of victims will never – ever – work with the TJRC no matter the competence of its bureaucrats.

Book competence has no purchase in a truth commission that the public believes is a whitewash. The work of a TJRC is not accountancy. It’s a social and public relations calling.

That’s why it does not matter how many mirages Mr Kiplagat creates. The logs in his eyes are still there – and visibly so. Let me remind you of them.

First, he is listed in the Ndung’u Report as a recipient of illegally allocated land. He has admitted being a beneficiary of the allocation.

Second, he was in government service when Dr Robert Ouko, the Foreign Affairs minister, was murdered in 1990. He has not been forthcoming about what he knew and did, or did not do.

Third, his name has come up in conversations about the Wagalla Massacre. His denials have been unconvincing. There’s more, but these are the most damning allegations. Yet Mr Kiplagat does not give a damn.

How, on God’s earth, can someone with such a cloud over them chair a truth commission? Mr Kiplagat’s supporters charge that his critics are now barred from raising these allegations because they did not do so before he was appointed.

Don’t they know that new evidence can be introduced at any stage during a trial? Others claim that Mr Kiplagat’s opponents are piqued because they were not appointed to the TJRC. This is silly because Ms Murungi herself was a commissioner.

Besides, most of the critics are gainfully employed. These juvenile defences of Mr Kiplagat should stop. He is the one who is in the dock, not his critics.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC*
