# Who’s afraid of Willy Mutunga at the helm of Judiciary? 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2011-05-21T14:12:07Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/who-s-afraid-of-willy-mutunga-at-the-helm-of-judiciary--769450

## What you need to know:

* Kenyans are firmly behind the nominee for Chief Justice and only the guilty need be afraid, very afraid

Chief Justice nominee Willy Mutunga has taken the country by storm. President Mwai Kibaki and PM Raila Odinga have promptly and wisely endorsed him. Speaker Kenneth Marende, an emerging sage, has embraced Dr Mutunga. So have Energy minister Kiraitu Murungi and PNU.

But we are not home and dry — yet. An unholy alliance of anti-reformers led by Eldoret North MP and NCCK General Secretary Peter Karanja want to shoot Dr Mutunga down. This is the ‘No’ campaign that opposed the new Constitution.

The “Reds” and some of the “watermelons” are back. Which begs the question — why are they afraid of Dr Mutunga? Methinks they want to torpedo reforms and neuter the new Constitution. News flash — they’ll fail miserably.

Anti-reformers are back to their old tricks — use the courts to kill change and thwart justice. That’s why lawyer Harrison Kinyanjui sought to stop Dr Mutunga at the High Court. The court rebuffed him. Make no mistake — the “Reds” will now attempt subterfuge in Parliament.

**The kitchen sink**

Like Dracula, they want to suck the blood out of us. They will throw everything, including the kitchen sink, at Dr Mutunga. I know why. They are gasping for air, and kicking their last. They can hear the drumbeat of history, and they don’t like what they are hearing.

The bell is tolling, and they know it tolls for them. But they should know the game is up. Their torment of Kenya ends now.

Let me address three silly — and evil — arguments proffered against Dr Mutunga. The first has to do with an ornament of dress. This is the “famous” stud.

Malignant detractors see “Armageddon” in the stud. Some think it’s a symbol of gay sexuality. Others opine that it shows the man as godless at worst, and un-Christian, at best. Yet some, like Mr Ruto, think it bespeaks of Dr Mutunga’s “evil connection” to his African ancestors.

Wow — talk about clutching at straws. These postulates would be laughable were they not so evil. It’s the kind of putrid public discourse that lowers Kenya’s collective IQ to unacceptably dangerous levels. Interrogate Dr Mutunga’s judicial philosophy, but leave the man’s stud alone.

**A new low**

Even for Mr Ruto and the sections of the clergy, this is a new low. Even for Mr Ruto, a propagandist prone to rhetorical licence, this is a stretch. How well does he know Dr Mutunga to make such outrageous and inane claims?

I am shocked Mr Ruto imagines himself a serious presidential candidate with such a sordid imagination. I know Dr Mutunga like few others do. But I am not the only one who knows this living legend.

I don’t know of too many people who can hold a candle to him on personal integrity and rectitude. He has mentored countless Kenyans while denying himself the trappings of power and money.

The second “evil” charge is that Dr Mutunga is gay. I take real umbrage at this shameless play to smear one of the greatest champions of human rights with homophobic slurs. First — and I will say this only once — Dr Mutunga isn’t gay.

But — and this is more important — sexual orientation has nothing to do with the price of beans in China. Who a person sleeps with has no bearing whatsoever on their ability to be the Chief Justice, or to do any other job for that matter.

Judges, like other professionals and thinkers, use their brains to do their work. No other parts of the human anatomy are germane to judging.

This sort of ignorance is intolerable.

Frankly, I wouldn’t give a whit even if Dr Mutunga was gay. Gay people are human beings who deserve our respect and protection. Perhaps the Rev Karanja should tell us what kind of God preaches hate, and not love, for all his children.

That’s not the Christian God that I am familiar with. I have said before — and will do so again — that sexual orientation is a fact of nature. I am sure the Rev Karanja and other homophobes didn’t choose to be straight — if that’s what they are — although quite frankly I don’t care.

Nor would a stud on a man’s ear indicate sexual orientation. If so, many Kenyans — Maasai, Kikuyu, Kamba and Turkana, to mention a few — would be gay because of their “earrings”.

Finally, the “Mutunga haters” suggest that he’s not Christian, or “Christian enough”. Hello — since when did Kenya, which is a secular republic, require that its Chief Justice be a Christian, or a believer of any religion?

The Chief Justice isn’t a pastor or mullah. He doesn’t minister at a church, synagogue, mosque or any spiritual shrine. Nor is Kenya’s Judiciary a monastery.

**Temple of justice**

It is a “temple” of justice that isn’t infested with spirits. Its only guide is the Constitution and the law. An atheist could be a judge. How do we know — there may even be atheists serving in the current Judiciary.

My point is that Dr Mutunga’s religiosity — or lack of it — is utterly irrelevant.

Parliament should know that Kenya has endorsed Dr Mutunga. Never before in my lifetime has a single appointment so galvanised the country.  
I know Kenyans dare the “Reds” to torpedo Dr Mutunga’s nomination. They haven’t seen the people’s fury.

The reaction of Kenyans will make the Arab Spring look mild. We know the lords of impunity are quaking in their boots. But they should fear not because the Judiciary, under Dr Mutunga, will be fair and just.

Only the guilty need to be afraid. That’s why MPs must approve Dr Mutunga.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
