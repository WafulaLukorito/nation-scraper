# Kibaki&apos;s best kept secret

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2007-04-06T21:00:00Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/news/kibaki-s-best-kept-secret-532014  

---

President Kibaki’s supporters and close allies have been thrown into uncertainty and confusion as the Head of State continues to keep his party of choice for this year’s General Election a top secret.

President Kibaki

The Democratic Party of Kenya, Narc Kenya and the original Narc have all expressed interest in fielding President Kibaki when he seeks a second term at State House in December.

But the President has kept a studious silence over the matter, prompting a state of confusion, panic and even infighting among some of his supporters.

The infighting in Narc-K which saw Trade minister Mukhisa Kituyi removed as party chairman and the quiet re-emergence of the Democratic Party, are some of the manifestations of the confusion among his backers.

Although President Kibaki’s key allies never lifted fingers against each other when they were in DP, some of them are now accusing each other of all manner of ills.

The infighting is most evident in Central Kenya, where any aspirant who gets the nomination of the party which President Kibaki will select will almost certainly be assured of election as MP. Take Sports minister Maina Kamanda and Education assistant minister Beth Mugo for instance. Both key supporters of the President from the DP days, they are today in opposite camps in the Narc-K grassroots elections row. Other Kibaki supporters who have found themselves in opposing camps are Cabinet ministers Kiraitu Murungi, Mwangi Kiunjuri and Chris Murungaru, on one side, against their former allies Joshua Toro, Nderitu Gachagua, David Mwenje, Jayne Kihara and Murue Kariuki.

Kanu chief whip Justin Muturi, who described the group as having been “one sheep” during the DP days, said lack of a unifying factor was making them “get lost in the wilderness”.

A vocal crusader for Narc-Kenya, Mr Njeru Githae, an assistant minister for Transport and Ndia MP, has warned that bickering in the party could “scare off” the President and make him seek a ticket from a host of parties that are friendly to him. Mr Githae said DP and the original Narc were ready to offer President Kibaki a ticket for the State House race.

A close associate of President Kibaki, who did not want to be identified for fear of antagonising his colleagues, said the Head of State was not about to make his preferred party known until he dissolves Parliament.

He said that political advisers had warned the President that it would be suicidal to make known his choice of party now “because he would immediately be branded a defector from the party on whose ticket he rode to power”.

But he also disclosed that President Kibaki was always in consultation with “friendly parties”.

The President has his finger in several agreeable parties. If he was to make a choice it would not be a difficult one. In DP, his friend Mr Munyao is secretary-general. In Narc, Mr Alex Mureithi is his ally. And most MPs in Narc-Kenya support him.

Mr Munyao has declared that the President would defend his seat on the party’s ticket. Narc-Kenya leaders have always made it known that the President was their man, while the original Narc too has put in its claim on him as its candidate.

However, one may want to dismiss Narc, because of Health minister Charity Ngilu’s sometimes fierce criticism of the Government. Mr Mureithi, the President’s nephew, has managed Mr Kibaki’s parliamentary and presidential campaigns for years and has never spoken about Narc-Kenya.

But Co-operatives minister Njeru Ndwiga says the President is certainly in Narc-K. “It is clear where he is because his own vice-president and many of his Cabinet ministers cannot be guessing on his stand,” he said.

Commenting on President Kibaki’s position, political analyst and law professor Makau Mutua said: “Narc-Kenya is a rudderless, directionless, and listing ship that is destined for catastrophe because it lacks a captain.

“Clearly, no leader in Narc-K has the gravitas to lead it. Only President Kibaki, on whose sole name the party was founded, can give it some direction.” But he blamed the President for the fallout in the National Rainbow Coalition (Narc).

“There was infighting in Narc because of the vacuum created by President Kibaki. There is infighting in Narc-K because President Kibaki has created a vacuum by failing to lead.”

He said the President could not claim to be above party politics when parties were the vehicles through which to conduct politics.

Water minister Mutua Katuku and assistant ministers Kiunjuri and Danson Mungatana said the President’s stand was the best because he was a leader of a Government of National Unity.

And since the President required the support of all parties in the GNU in his re-election bid, he needed to stay above party politics.

The three said President Kibaki’s party of choice was Narc-Kenya because he had campaigned for its aspirant in Nakuru Town during last year’s by-elections.

Said Mr Katuku: “It is a good strategy. He will get more supporters if he is not associated with one particular party.”

But his Cabinet colleague, Mr Soita Shitanda, argued that the infighting in Narc-Kenya had nothing to do with the President. He said there was a group of MPs from Central Kenya who were unpopular and feared that if elections were called now they would lose to aspiring candidates.

Mr Shitanda’s view was supported by Mr Mungatana who said that a few individuals in the party had problems in their constituencies.

Mr Kiunjuri said Narc Kenya was steeped in wrangles because some individuals were fighting for supremacy.

Mr Nderitu Gachagua, said it was not obvious that President Kibaki would seek re-election through the party.

“That is why we are saying there is no need for elections at this time. We have to move out and popularise the party to make it have a national outlook,” he said.

Mr Gachagua said the President could be keeping people in suspense because he had his political battle to consider and the damage it would cost him if he was to hurriedly declare his party of choice.

Ntonyiri MP Maoka Maore (Kanu) argued that the revival of DP — which is holding countrywide polls — is because it wants to fill the vacuum created by Narc-K wars and get the President’s attention.

The confusion over the President’s party of choice has been compounded by the fact that his pointmen in Central — Cabinet ministers Njenga Karume, John Michuki and Kamanda — have not been speaking out.

**Ongoing wrangles**

It is noteworthy that Mr Karume and Mr Michuki have notably kept off the ongoing wrangles about parties probably because only they in Central know where Mr Kibaki really stands on the issue.

Kanu chief whip Muturi said President Kibaki is not going to declare his party soon because he fears the consequences. “Since when did you see him come to the open on such matters?” he asked.

He agreed with Prof Mutua that it was the President’s style not to intervene in such matters until “they settled themselves”.

“His lieutenants are terribly confused but he will let them tear into each other until eventually they find a solution,” Mr Muturi said. Eventually, he added, the President will support the way forward after an agreement has been reached.
