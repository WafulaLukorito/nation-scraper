A lobby group Thursday asked a court to halt the recruitment of a new chief justice and the deputy.

Trusted Society for Human Rights Alliance has filed a petition in the High Court in Nakuru seeking to stop the ongoing recruitment of candidates to fill the posts.

The lobby, through lawyers Benard Kipkoech Ng’etich and Lawrence Karanja, claims that the recruitment “is opaque, clandestine and shrouded in secrecy and does not inspire public confidence”.

The alliance has sued the Judicial Service Commission claiming that the body was resistant to public scrutiny, and is in the process of midwifing the choice of the chief justice, deputy chief justice and the Supreme Court judge posts based on an unknown criteria.

The petitioner claims that the commission shortlisted candidates for those positions in secrecy against the Constitution.

“The recruitment is not constitutional as it has been turned into a theatre of absurdities. It is not open to public participation,” reads part of the petition.

The alliance also claims that the process has denied participants an equal opportunity as required by the law.

The elimination of law professor Makau Mutua, Supreme Court Judge Jackton Ojwang and former anti-graft boss Aaron Ringera, the lobby alleges, was driven by malice and greed on the part of the JSC members.

The lobby is, therefore, seeking orders barring the Judiciary from interviewing the shortlisted candidates and filling the positions until the matter is heard and determined.

Judge Janet Mulwa set July 21 for interparte hearing. Interviews for the posts are scheduled for July 29.
