## What you need to know:

* Only through a superior and intelligent mind can one fight inner complexes of self-destruction and inferior emotions.
* It’s true that most of us suffer from one form of trauma or disability, even if we may be unaware of it.

In 1972, the United Negro College Fund, an African-American charity devoted to supporting the education of Blacks, coined the tagline “A mind is a terrible thing to waste.” The idea of the fund was to make sure African-Americans got access to education to allow them to exercise full rights of citizenship. Only through education can one have a chance to realise one’s full human potential.

Through it, and because of it, one can combat ignorance, dehumanisation, and other forms of oppression. The mind is the most powerful tool for freeing self and community. That’s why it’s a terrible thing to waste it. Only through a superior and intelligent mind can one fight inner complexes of self-destruction and inferior emotions.

The superior and intelligent being must constantly engage in a personal battle, an intensely internal combustion of the human mind to ceaselessly purify the self and jettison pettiness, “evil” proclivities, and other inferior emotions. This individualised struggle from within must be taught both by self and society. It requires a ruthless and unforgiving self-criticism and an unlimited ambition to strive for absolute humility. For none of us knows it all. We fall short all the time, but we must pick ourselves up every time and continue the fight until our time on the planet expires. We cannot tire, or give up, much less give in. We need to develop and hone internal “speedometers” for those triggers that reduce our emotional intelligence.

We must educate ourselves about the self. To be able to recognise our deficits as individuals. To stop flattering ourselves. To reduce the humanity in the other person. To tame our greed and selfishness. To do so, we must understand our weaknesses. If we think of the human mind as having four compartments (the black box, the box known to us, the box only known to others, and the box known to everyone) then we can start the process of self-actualization. This will require reducing the black box – the reservoir of ignorance – which is the largest part in every human brain. The purpose of education must be defined broadly because there are many ways knowledge – formal and informal – is to shrink the box of stupidity, idiocy, and ignorance.

## Complex of inferiority

There are several ways of developing a higher human intelligence. The first is to know thyself, especially your emotional weaknesses and trigger points. What are your insecurities? Do you suffer from panic and petty jealousies? Do you feel the urge to project strength to dominate others? Do you know, or realise, when you want to engage in gratuitous violence? Do you especially get joy from bullying others or harassing folks you consider weaker than you? In the workplace do you kiss up, and kick down? In other words, do you pick on “little people?” Do you with the anonymity of the internet and social media exhibit – obsessively – these aggressions against other people? Conversely, do you compliment others for a job well done?

It’s true that most of us suffer from one form of trauma or disability, even if we may be unaware of it. Traumas result in all sorts of psychosis, including post-traumatic stress disorders. Some of these can be very severe and drive us to harm others, or self. See a trained medical professional if you feel weird. They can help you get therapy and understand yourself better before it’s too late. I consider anger an inferior emotion, for example. Anger, which is a natural emotion, has been the cause of untold suffering. Learn to govern your anger, instead of letting it governor you. Don’t surrender to your anger. But don’t suppress it – instead conquer it by managing it.

Complexes of inferiority are equally destructive. People may drive you to self-hatred and to a complex of inferiority. This can happen if you are bullied, or if you are regarded as less than pleasing to the naked eye. Perhaps you are overweight, or not easy on the eyes. Perhaps people always overlook you and seem to gravitate to the person next to you. This can happen because of colourism, especially among people of Black African descent, who have been victimised by racism and in turn victimise each other by elevating lighter skin to a more desirable pigmentation. This also happens with hair. Those with “black hair” – pejoratively called “nappy” hair – have been so repressed that they in turn they repress themselves.

Finally, let me return to my basic argument. Self-awareness should be one of the key purposes of any educational system. This is especially true on identity and the inner combat with the self. Fighting your demons, and not becoming one yourself, ought to be a critical goal of knowing and knowledge. Unless you know yourself, you won’t achieve even optimum happiness. Taking refuge in religion or marauding conduct won’t help you. Projecting, or demeaning others, is a cul de sac. Only an honest confrontation with your fears and inadequacies can set you free.
