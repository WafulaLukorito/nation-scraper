# Ocampo Six: Let the law take its course 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2010-12-17T14:55:20Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/ocampo-six-let-the-law-take-its-course--749790  

---

The “Gang of Six” is toast. Do not believe the bravado and braggadocio of Deputy Prime Minister Uhuru Kenyatta and Cabinet Secretary Francis Muthaura.

Nor should you pay any attention to the empty boasts of Eldoret North MP William Ruto.

The three – together with Industrialisation Minister Henry Kosgey, former Commissioner of Police Hussein Ali, and *KASS FM*’s Joshua arap Sang – are scared witless. Who wouldn’t?

Crimes against humanity are the vilest of wrongs. It’s up there with genocide. That’s why, no matter the spin, the Gang of Six is “finished”.

My crystal ball tells me – and you can take this to the bank -- Luis Moreno-Ocampo, the ICC prosecutor, has amassed a mountain of evidence. Don’t doubt it.

Why do I think so? The stakes are simply too high, and Mr Moreno-Ocampo is way too smart to gamble on flimsy evidence.

Get this. Why would Mr Moreno-Ocampo go after Mr Kenyatta without a smoking gun? Mr Kenyatta is the heart and soul of Kenya’s political class. He is the quintessential establishment figure.

He was destined to end up at State House. That, in all probability, is now a shattered dream. Instead, he must now spend sleepless nights under the guillotine of Mr Moreno-Ocampo.

Mr Moreno-Ocampo has cooked Mr Kenyatta’s goose. My point is that you don’t take on the tallest oak in the forest unless you have the tools to bring it down.  
To paraphrase President Mwai Kibaki, Mr Kenyatta is a *mugumo* (fig) tree, but Mr Moreno-Ocampo is not a razor blade. I am sure the son of Jomo will bring the full weight of his family largesse to bear.

He will have the finest lawyers and the most suave publicists. That’s why he’s already playing Mr Cool. Mr Muthaura, aka President Kibaki’s alter ego, is a different matter. The reclusive bureaucrat has been devastated by the charges.

He must rue why he didn’t, as he reportedly planned, retire before the 2007 elections. But I guess alter egos exist to take one for the boss. It remains to be seen whether Mr Muthaura will cut a deal to save himself.

The other fat fish netted by Mr Moreno-Ocampo is Mr Ruto. But the combative legislator has run out of options. He mistakenly thought that the ICC was a public relations charade. But he also believed he could cloak himself in an ethnic garb to scare Mr Moreno-Ocampo off.

I could have told him – for free – that the Argentine would call off his bluff. Then he tried to “attack the evidence” by claiming that witnesses had been “manufactured” against him. That, too, fell, like a dud.

Now he will have no option but to appear in Mr Moreno-Ocampo’s temple of justice. If I was Mr Ruto, I would make no further public statements. When in a hole, don’t dig deeper.

To some, Mr Muthaura was a surprise. So was Mr Kosgey, although there were street rumours. The mild mannered Tinderet legislator is a veteran of Rift Valley politics. A vanguard of the Moi regime, Mr Kosgey remade himself into an ally of PM Raila Odinga.

But, apparently, Mr Moreno-Ocampo believes that Mr Kosgey was too enthusiastic of an ODM kingpin. He is accused of planning mayhem – murder, rape, and looting – to put ODM in power.

Then there is Mr Sang, the radio journalist accused of tribal incitement. His may be an open and shut case because the tapes of his utterances are publicly available. Mr Moreno-Ocampo has likely taken down titans of Kenya’s political *nomenclatura.*

I understand President Kibaki’s rehearsed homage about “innocent until proven guilty”. But when the charge is crimes against humanity, such a statement from the head of state is spurious and unwarranted.

Why is he defending the Gang of Six? Shouldn’t he let the law take its course? Or was he simply playing to the gallery without meaning a word?

Mr Kibaki shouldn’t cast doubt on the ICC process, or appear to undermine it. Instead, he and PM Raila Odinga should immediately relieve Mr Kenyatta, Mr Kosgey, and Mr Muthaura of their duties.

Kenya cannot have senior officials charged with crimes against humanity in office. They must go! Mr Moreno-Ocampo has wreaked havoc with both PNU and ODM. But PNU has been hit the hardest.

Why? Because the KKK alliance of Mr Kenyatta, Mr Ruto, and VP Kalonzo Musyoka has lost two of its strongest pillars. Mr Kenyatta and Mr Ruto are, for all practical purposes, out of commission for 2012.

This is bad news for Mr Musyoka because he alone cannot marshal the Kikuyu and Kalenjin. Which begs the question – does PNU have any other plausible candidate apart from Mr Musyoka to take on Mr Odinga, the presumptive ODM presidential nominee?

My crystal ball tells me that PNU will implode under the weight of Mr Moreno-Ocampo’s predations. It has no path to victory in 2012.

Kenyans should delight at the taking down of the Gang of Six. This is not about tribe, but country. Mr Moreno-Ocampo, a Latin American, may very well save Kenya. This could be the end of the culture of impunity.

It could slay the dragon of ethnic demagoguery. I have a word for Mr Odinga and Mr Kibaki. Gentlemen, measure carefully the actions you take and the words you utter going forward.

What you do, or say, could make or break Kenya. Let the Gang of Six bear its own cross. Let the ICC do its job.

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
