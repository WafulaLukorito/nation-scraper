# We&apos;ll not sack Ringera, Karua tells critics

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2006-09-27T21:00:00Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/news/we-ll-not-sack-ringera-karua-tells-critics-522990  

---

The Government yesterday refused to bow to demands to sack anti-corruption head Aaron Ringera.

And rubbing salt in the wound, Justice minister Martha Karua dismissed ODM Kenya leaders who organised demonstrations demanding Ringera's dismissal as, "prime suspects of past corruption scandals and other economic crimes."

Orange Democratic Movement supporters demonstrate in the streets of Nairobi yesterday against anti-corruption czar Aaron Ringera. Photo/Joseph Mathenge

Her comments came as the reasons emerged for the Government's last minute decision to allow the Nairobi demonstration to go ahead.

The decision was taken after high level consultations between Internal Security minister John Michuki and State House during which it was agreed that banning the protest march was likely to provoke a confrontation between the police and ODM-K supporters that could have turned the capital into a battlefield.

It was also felt the ODM-K leaders would capitalise on the violence to tarnish the Government's name both at home and abroad.

The peaceful demonstration yesterday was led by the party's interim chairman Henry Kosgey, MP for Tinderet, and MPs William Ruto (Eldoret North), Reuben Ndolo (Makadara), Otieno Kajwang' (Mbita), Ayiecho Olweny (Muhoroni), William Omondi (Kasarani), Ochola Ogur (Nyatike) and Peter Odoyo (Nyakach). None of the party's most prominent leaders was present. They included Mr Raila Odinga and Professor Anyang' Nyong'o, who were abroad, and Mr Kalonzo Musyoka who was mourning his mother.

Other high profile leaders who missed the march included Mr Musalia Mudavadi, Mr Najib Balala and Mr Mutula Kilonzo.

Ms Karua announced the Government's refusal to give in to the ODM-K demands at a Press conference she called moments after returning to her office following consultations with State House.

"What credible Government would act on a petition by suspects, demanding the removal of those investigating them?" she asked.

She added: "To give in to such demands would otherwise mean next time, even robbers would organise members of their families to demonstrate in the streets to stop certain judges from hearing their cases."

The demonstration to demand the resignation of Mr Ringera or the appointment of a tribunal to investigate his conduct, Ms Karua said, was aimed at intimidating the Kenya Anti-Corruption Commission into slowing down its investigations into alleged misdeeds by some of the ODM-K leaders.

"I want to express my dismay at the demonstration by the ODM against Justice Ringera because it is a sad day in the history of this country where people known to be under investigation gang up to intimidate the investigating agency which is after them," she said.

And Ms Karua said the threat to physically to throw out Mr Ringera from his office amounted to incitement to violence.

**List of Shame**

"Kenyans should judge for themselves the calibre of leaders who are saying they are fit to lead the country while at the same time inciting people to lawlessness," she went on.

The minister suggested it was not a coincidence that the threats to evict Mr Ringera were made during an ODM-K rally in Kisumu at the weekend, only a few days after she released the List of Shame of those said to be involved in the Anglo Leasing type scandals.

She singled out Lang'ata MP Raila Odinga, who was one of the speakers at the Kisumu rally, as allegedly having an axe to grind with the anti-graft body.

"Who doesn't know that Raila has moved to court to try and stop the Kenya Anti-Corruption Commission from investigating his family over the acquisition of the Kisumu Molasses?" she asked. She declared the Government would live up to its promise to make public more names of people allegedly involved in corruption and other forms of plunder of public resources.

"They can run but they will not hide and I can assure them that they are in a for a shock because we shall definitely take action on them," she said.

The minister went on to ask former Cabinet minister William Ole Ntimama to forward any details he might have over one of the Anglo Leasing-type of contracts that is documented as having been signed during his tenure.

Mr Ntimama has queried why President Kibaki's special adviser Mr Stanley Murage was not named on the List of Shame even though he was permanent secretary in the Ministry of Transport when the contract was signed.

Mr Murage has denied signing any such contract.

Said Ms Karua: "This is not a child's game and let us be serious. If he knows where the money went, let him tell us and he should not forget it is also a crime to abet theft."

In a petition presented during their demonstration yesterday, the ODM-K wanted Mr Ringera indicted for alleged dereliction of duty, abuse of trust, inertia, partiality, and fraudulently earning public money when no services had been rendered.

**Velvet gloves**

ODM-K was concerned by his "demonstrated inability to firmly implement, enforce and actualise the full extent of the statutory mandate of the commission, by his penchant to handle with velvet gloves any corruption allegations touching on the politically correct, his apparent fatal dependence on the political patronage of some actors in the Kibaki government, his inability to draw a firm line between petty vindictive politics and the solemn anti-graft war."

The party said it was gravely alarmed by allegations of impropriety made against him by Mr John Githongo, lawyer Prof Makau Mutua and former commission investigator Hussein Were.

The allegations, the party said, had eroded Kenyans' confidence in him as a standard bearer in the war on corruption.

Attempts to contact Mr Ringera for comment were fruitless.
