# NGOs say new line-up of Cabinet is a liability

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2004-07-02T21:00:00Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/news/ngos-say-new-line-up-of-cabinet-is-a-liability-724312  

---

A number of organisations have criticised the new Cabinet line-up, describing it as a liability to the tax-payer and a disappointment.

And they accused President Mwai Kibaki of attempting to solve the wrangling in Narc through an inflated Cabinet and at the expense of tax-payers.

"The Cabinet is too big; it's not cost effective and is unlikely to be efficient,'' they said.

"We believe the President should not solve the problems in his government at the expense of taxpayers who will now have to meet the enormous cost of paying for this inflated government,'' said the consortium of prominent non-governmental organisations (NGOs).

Describing the 31-member Cabinet as a disappointment, they recalled that President Kibaki had, when naming his first Cabinet, promised the nation a lean, efficient and cost-effective line-up.

Said five NGO representatives during a joint news conference at the Transparency International (TI) in Nairobi: "The Kenyan leadership is rapidly losing the goodwill and trust of the people because it is unable to keep commitments and promises.''

Their statement was signed by the NGOs' executive directors Koki Muli (Institute for Education in Democracy), Gladwell Otieno (TI), Jane Kiragu (Federation of Women Lawyers - Kenya), Wanjiku Miano (Kenya Human Rights Commission) and Phillip Kichana (International Commission of Jurists - Kenya)

Separately, Prof Makau Mutua, the Kenya Human Rights Commission (KHRC) chairman, described the new Cabinet line-up as "a disappointment and a mortal blow to reformers and the civil society who have fought for over a decade to recreate the Kenyan State."

He went on: "The reshuffle is a clear indication that the Kibaki government is very much like the Moi government it replaced. It is increasingly evident that Narc is only interested in retaining and husbanding power for its own sake, and not in using it to reform the country, '' said the law don.

He supported the idea of national unity, but said some Opposition politicians now in the Cabinet needed to answer for crimes against Kenyans.
