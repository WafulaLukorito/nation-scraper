# Debunking Makau’s myths on Mudavadi

**Date:** 2021-11-20T20:56:45Z

By 
[Kibisu Kabatesi](/kenya/blogs-opinion/blogs/kibisu-kabatesi-3625990)

My detail is to speak for Amani National Congress (ANC) leader Musalia Mudavadi, including on matters like [Prof Makau Mutua’s scornful attack on the “gentleman” from Mululu](https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/musalia-mudavadi-the-overlooked-gentleman-3618020) (‘Mudavadi, the overlooked gentleman’, Sunday Nation , November 14, 2021). In my spokesman function, I try not to be obstinate, bullying and needy in thought; though I could be rough in dealing with intellectual malcontents like Makau.

This is the second time an intellectually thinning Makau has set out to malign Musalia in a ruse that camouflages a campaign for his favourite presidential aspirant. The scandalous one in April 2021 was spared a right of reply cryptically embellished in allegory thus: ‘Makau Mutua endorses hyena to be President of Kenya in 2022.’

The editor “felt” the imagery was unpalatable as  it sponged the person of Makau off his pretentions. Pretentions that somehow, he’s a persona in the multiparty struggle who never got a just reward, and pretentions to an intellectual powerhouse not given due recognition by various regimes.

I reiterate my April prognosis that Makau has become stale, redundant, cheap, petty and repugnant to good sense. His choice for President would, for example, be determined by the record of jail stints. He dismisses qualities of good leadership on fanciful grounds to satisfy his incessant search to be accommodated in some political sanctum, where he hopes to experiment his washed-out academic superstition projects.

Intellectual decline is usually marked by insolence; escape into fantasy and profanity; and a sycophantic fireside prattle told in pedestrian pseudo analysis. Lately, Makau has veered from issues-based content to character assassination.

In the Sunday Nation of November 14, Makau embarked on a fallacious journey to disrespectfully discover Musalia Mudavadi – as man, son, brother, husband, father and politician he hardly knows. He ended up faking a “phantom” Musalia we could hardly recognise from that nickname diligently acquired through rugby prowess.

Reality doesn’t bother Makau as much as the fictitious characters he foists on Kenyans. He takes a plunge into a deceptive description of Musalia, trashing all his positive attributes.

## Fairy tale

But you know Makau hasn’t read a nit bit of Soaring Above the Storms of Passion, the autobiography of his hate figure, when he invents a fairy tale that Musalia “was born with a silver spoon in his mouth, never known hardship, or penury”.

This is deliberate as Makau must deconstruct Musalia to prop up his hero – Raila Odinga.

Luckily, an educated Kenyan reviewing the autobiography says Musalia “comes out as a bold and brave person”. The book is, in the perceptive words of Pheroze Nowrojee, “a Kenyan journey, as told through the experiences of an individual...”

Khainga O’Okwemba’s book review of January 2020 digs deeper into Musalia we know: “His deportment is that of a firm but diligent person. Mudavadi is unflappable, even in the face of our chaliced politics... Indeed, he warns us about the betrayal and deception among the characters that populate the political space.”

A sulky Makau is in the ‘betrayal and deception’ league.  Makau patronisingly pretends to know the Luhya leadership matrix by trivialising its recognised leaders.  I invite Makau to cross the Atlantic mental void that blurs the immigrant’s memory of reality in their country of birth.

Statistics don’t lie; without being on a presidential ticket, Musalia built a three-month-old ANC in 2017 into the dominant party in western region.

Reluctantly, Makau credits Musalia for striding off the UhuRuto deception in 2013 to the ballot. I wonder how many hardened politicians can shrug off such cruel betrayal.

Makau knows Musalia refused a lucrative nomination in 2002 and has shoved away advances to serve in the Jubilee Cabinet for nine years now. That’s moral character. But that wouldn’t fit in Makau’s choreographed narrative.

*The writer is Private Secretary and Spokesman for Musalia Mudavadi*
