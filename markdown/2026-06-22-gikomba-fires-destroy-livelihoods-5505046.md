**Fishy roadblocks** • For months, Damson Opiyo Onger says, motorists along the Mau Summit–Kisumu highway have faced temporary police roadblocks that appear and disappear within hours. Wondering why “officers mount roadblocks for only two hours before dismantling them”, he questions “how such short-lived roadblocks enhance security, if not to harass motorists?” His contact is ***ongerdamson@gmail.com.***

\*\*\*

**Broken care** • Kiprotich Dennis says devolution promised closer healthcare but many counties have turned treatment into a gamble. “A hungry and demoralised medic cannot offer quality care,” he says, citing delayed salaries, stalled promotions and recurring strikes. He also cites drug shortages, theft and grounded ambulances, calling hospitals “empty shells” and urging reforms.

\*\*\*

**Gikomba fires** • Samuel Kararî Chege says Gikomba Market has for years been hit by mysterious fires. He describes it as the “JKIA of mitumba”, serving thousands of traders in second-hand clothing. “It is never clear how the fires start,” he says, faulting authorities for failing to modernise and organise the market. He cites poor lighting and infrastructure, saying the fires destroy livelihoods.

\*\*\*

**Peaceful protests** • Vallentine Mmbone says recent violence, including attack on worshippers at a cathedral by suspected goons, signals growing intolerance in public spaces. He urges protesters to act peacefully and lawfully. “Citizens should be able to express themselves without fear,” warning that violence, destruction of property and intimidation undermine legitimate grievances.

**Piñata politics** • Githuku Mungai says Dr Makau Mutua described Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua as a “piñata” in last week’s Sunday Nation and urged readers to look up its meaning.
