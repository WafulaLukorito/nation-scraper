## What you need to know:

* The position of Supreme Court judge which fell vacant after Justice Philip Tunoi retired has attracted 21 candidates.
* The Judicial Service Commission on Thursday announced that it will convene on Monday to open the applications.

The vacancy for the position of chief justice has attracted 14 applicants.

By the end of the deadline Thursday, two female candidates, Lady Justice Roselyne Nambuye and Ms Lucy Wanja Julius had submitted their applications and will now battle it out with 12 male candidates for the post, which became vacant after Dr Willy Mutunga retired last month.

The male candidates include judges Smokin Wanjala, Prof Jackton Ojwang, Alnashir Visram, David Maraga, Mbogholi Msagha, and Rtd Justice Aaron Ringera.

Other male candidates include Prof Makau Mutua, David Mwaure Waihiga, Dr Isaac Rutenberg, Philip Nzamba Kitonga, Kongani Udoto Kongani, Paul Andrew and Ambassador Daniel Wambura.

The position of deputy chief justice attracted 14 female candidates and one male candidate. It fell vacant after Justice Kalpana Rawal retired.

They include lady justices Hannah Okwengu, Abida Ali Aroni, Agnes Murgor, Wanjiru Karanja, Philomena Mwilu, Fatuma Sichale, Lydia Achode, Pauline Nyamweya, Martha Koome, and Roselyne Nambuye.

The non-judges for the position of DCJ are Surinder Kapila, Pamela Mwikali Tutui, Joyce Miguda Majiwa, Judith Wanjala and the male candidate, David Mwaure Waihiga.

The position of Supreme Court judge which fell vacant after Justice Philip Tunoi retired has attracted 21 candidates.

They include Lady Justices Martha Koome, Agnes Murgor, Wanjiru Karanja, Philomena Mwilu, Roselyne Nambuye, Fatuma Sichale, and Pauline Nyamweya.

The male candidates who are judges are Mbogholi Msagha, Alnashir Visram, Isaac Lenaola, Joseph Kiplangat Sergon, Erastus Githinji, and Luka Kimaru.

The non-judges seeking to be Supreme Court judge include Judith Wanjala, David Mwaure Waihiga, Prof Kevin Faustine O. Mare, Mr John Chebii Kipkoech, Ms Pamela Mwikali Tutui, Dr Kibaya Laibuta, Dr Isaac Rutenberg, and Ms Joyce Miguda Majiwa.

The Judicial Service Commission on Thursday announced that it will convene on Monday to open the applications.

The Commission will review applications for completeness and conformity with the necessary requirements within 14 days and thereafter start the process of interviewing the candidates.
