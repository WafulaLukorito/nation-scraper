# Prof Mutua, avoid this compensation sham

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2025-08-18T21:00:00Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/letters/prof-mutua-avoid-this-compensation-sham-5159092  

---

Professor Makau Mutua and I come from a community with a proverbial saying that loosely translates to “he who enjoys the company of people with skin diseases, will soon catch the disease.

I have had a lot of respect for Prof Mutua, having been a forerunner in the crusade for the respect of people and human rights. He was appointed in mid-April 2002 to join and head the Task Force on the Establishment of a Truth, Justice and Reconciliation Commission. On page 162 of his report, he recommended that the “task force should use its reading of the public mind, based on its public hearings, to recommend whether or not Kenya should establish a Truth Commission”.

Prof Mutua should heed his own words and tread with caution.

The public mood is that the establishment of a framework for compensation of victims of protests and riots, and the appointment of Prof Mutua to head it is dead on arrival. It is seen as another slap on the face of the victims and their families.

Prof Mutua is aware of so many reports, including the one by the Truth, Justice and Reconciliation Commission, which he midwifed. The Kenya National Commission on Human Rights, the principal body charged with the responsibility of promoting and protecting the Bill of Rights, has countless reports documenting human rights violations, with recommendations for redress. It is therefore boggling that Prof Mutua is still defending his position and desire to take up the appointment.

Why does the State want to rush to implement a framework for compensation when the investigations are bungled, some matters are in court, and some of the victims are still missing?

We know that the 2027 elections are near and that a lot is being done to “erase” records that might hurt re-election. The international community has also expressed concern about the bad human rights record, causing panic.

Prof Mutua should drop this engagement so that he is not seen as sanitising illegalities. I have spoken to many victims, and what they want is justice, not hurried moves to redeem institutions and individuals.

Those who killed protesters must be arrested and prosecuted. The courts will then award the victims and families whatever is just in a transparent manner.

**Lucas Kimanthi**
