# Why I fear Egypt could fall to al-Qaeda

## What you need to know:

* The assumption that democracy can’t co-exist with political Islam is utterly wrong.
* Arab Muslims displayed a great capacity for assimilating the scientific knowledge and the cultures they conquered.
* You can’t overturn a democratic regime and expect peace.

When I was a youngster in Kitui, my dad let me in on a little, but intellectually explosive, secret.

He looked me right in the eye – without blinking – and flatly said the Ancient Egyptians were Kamba. The truth – cross my heart and hope to die. Did he actually mean Ancient Egyptians – the ones who built the mighty pyramids and the Great Sphinx of Giza – were Kamba? He was dead serious.

I went gaga. Stunned. But hold on – before you come down hard on the man I reverently called “tata.” I’ve come to believe – with the wisdom of hindsight – that he was absolutely right. He was speaking metaphorically, not literally. He meant that Ancient Egyptians were black, not Arab.

Why is this important? I am worried – very worried. The magic country of my childhood dreams – the one my dad called Misri – may go to the dogs. I’m afraid the exotic land of Pharaoh Cleopatra, the enchantress who beguiled kings, is in deep doodoo. The cradle of civilisation is likely to fall to Islamic extremists – specifically al-Qaeda.

I can see al-Qaeda supremo Ayman Al-Zawahiri, rubbing his hands and salivating. That’s because the Egyptian military – with tacit winks from Washington – is making gargantuan errors of judgement. If Egypt falls – or turns into another Iraq, or Syria – it’ll be because its military and the West invited the catastrophe. Let me tell you what my crystal ball sees.

Extremists, whatever their stripe and hue, thrive where open dissent and popular will are extinguished. The great incubator of Islamic fanaticism isn’t political religion per se, but the inability of democrats to peacefully capture it.

Repressing political Islam only drives it underground, and makes it militant. That’s why Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Pakistan, and assorted Muslim countries have become petri-dishes for terrorists. It’s like a pressure-cooker – it will explode without a valve.

The assumption that democracy can’t co-exist with political Islam is utterly wrong. Look at Turkey – where it’s working. Or even Pakistan, where a democratic experiment is still alive, albeit very tenuous. My point is that you can’t wish political Islam away – smother and tame it with democratic norms.

It’s true that Islam missed the Age of Enlightenment. That’s the period in the 17th and 18th centuries when “reason” replaced “faith.” The philosopher Immanuel Kant said the Enlightenment emancipated humans from an “immature state of ignorance”. This was the genome for the Western secular democratic state.

Without it we wouldn’t have liberal political democracy – based on the bill of rights – as we know it today. The rupture between Christianity and politics – born of the Enlightenment – has no equivalent in Islam. That’s why political Islam has conceptual trouble with democracy. It’s the reason the Egyptian military and the West don’t trust the Muslim Brotherhood with power.

But repressing the Brotherhood will most likely turn it into a terrorist organisation.

Let me be clear. I totally reject the view, which the Egyptian military and the West hold, that Islam is inherently incompatible with political democracy. We know from Islam’s Golden Age – 8th to 13th centuries – that the Arab world was an intellectual centre for science, philosophy, education and medicine. More importantly, there was toleration of non-Muslim scholars.

Arab Muslims displayed a great capacity for assimilating the scientific knowledge and the cultures they conquered. It’s true they were imperialists, but so was the West before, during, and after the Enlightenment. Islam has normative wisdoms that are democratic.

It’s these that must be harvested. But that won’t happen if the West sees terrorists behind every Islamist political party.  
Which brings me back to Egypt. The Egyptian military tried in vain to stop the Brotherhood from winning the country’s first democratic election in 2012. Brotherhood leader Mohammed Morsy openly beat the military-backed Mubarak crony Ahmed Shafik in two rounds of free balloting.

Egyptian “liberals” – darlings of the West – were wiped out. It was clear that neither the West, nor the military and the “liberals,” would accept a Brotherhood victory. In July – after a little more than a year in office – President Morsy was overthrown by a conspiracy of the military and the “liberals.”

America refused to call this takedown a coup, effectively legitimising the putsch against a democratically elected leader. Mr Morsy, now a “martyr,” has been detained incommunicado.  
The military has carried out mass killings, arbitrary arrests, and a scorched campaign against the Muslim Brotherhood. The soldiers are doing through bullets what they couldn’t do through ballots.

Except the persecution of the Brotherhood won’t kill the group, Egypt’s best organised party. It will only drive the members underground and multiply their already overwhelming numbers. Methinks their next move will be guerrilla and suicide attacks.

The response of the military will be more repression. Egypt will then be plunged into a civil war. Former dictator Hosni Mubarak repressed the Brotherhood, but that was before the Arab Spring. Those tactics can’t – and won’t – work today.

You can’t overturn a democratic regime and expect peace.

I end where I started. The West needs to stop reflexive responses to electoral victories by Islamist parties. Fact – Muslim countries can become democratic. Malaysia, Indonesia and Senegal are exemplars. But that can only happen if all political parties – especially Islamist religious ones – are given an equal chance to compete in open elections.

Their victory must be respected once it’s earned. The only caveat is that they agree on irreducible core values – fidelity to democracy, equal protection and non-discrimination. Driving Islamist parties from power – when they win squarely and fairly – can only turn them into terrorists. That’s why Egypt may be lost.
