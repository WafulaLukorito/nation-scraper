# Wafula wins Open Strokeplay on Royal course

**Author:** Larry Ngala  
**Date:** 2014-06-16T19:51:52Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/sports/golf/wafula-wins-open-strokeplay-on-royal-course-994624  

---

By 
[Larry Ngala](/kenya/larry-ngala-1832)

Correspondent

NMG Sport

Kenya’s number one female golfer Naomi Wafula at the weekend won the Kenya Ladies Open Strokeplay golf championship title at Royal Nairobi Golf Club.

The 16-year-old Wafula, currently playing off handicap three, beat a field of over 100 golfers that had players from 17 countries ,which competed in the All Africa Challenge Trophy, to claim the overall title with a two-day total of 152.

She posted 77 gross in the first and 75 in the second. Winning the Silver division title with a total of 153 made up of 76 and 77 was Zambia’s handicap one golfer Melisa Nawa who beat Tanzania’s Madina Iddi by a shot and another Tanzanian Angel Eaton by two shots.

The Tanzanians took the second, third and fourth prizes as Hawa Wanyeche (157 gross) finished fourth.

Kenya’s Josephine Ainley was fifth also on 157, beaten by Wanyeche on countback. Rachna Patel won the Silver division nett title on 142.

She won by two shots from Sarah Otolo and Uganda’s Jenina Nasimolo was third on 149 after beating Sarah Hoare on countback.

**GUEST TITLE**

Taking the Bronze Division Gross title was Railway’s Gathigia  Gatere with 192 after beating Wanja Kimani of Royal on countback. Grace Wokabi won the nett title on 154 nett.

Rwanda’s Jenney Linda was second on 159 nett. Mary Monari from the Rose Naliaka Academy won the junior gross title on 159 and her colleague Sarah Kanyereri won the nett on 157.

At Machakos, Francis Njeru beat a field of 150 to win the Access Kenya-sponsored Chairman’s Prize. Playing off handicap eight, Njeru carded 42 points to beat second-placed Dee kivuva, C. Makau and Jackson Nzioki by two points. J. Mboya (39) was fifth and handicap three Annest Mwanza (37) sixth.

The guest title in the event, attended by Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua, went to Eldoret’s John Barorot with 41 points. He won by two points from A. Kitur while Kamau Watunu (38) came third. In fourth place was Peter Maina (37 points), a point better than sixth-placed J. Mugo.

The ladies were led by Jackline Koli on 34 points after a countback with Grace Koech. Joel Gachahu was the staff winner with 33 points and the nines went to Fred Kilonzi (20) and Esther Chumo (19).
