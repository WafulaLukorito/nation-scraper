## What you need to know:

* No national crisis anywhere has ever been solved by one individual, no matter how gifted.
* Our first and most serious national danger is existential, and it’s the youth bulge.
* Gen Zs think that they have the monopoly of wisdom.

They say that a doctor who treats himself has a fool for a patient. I recall this ominous admonition because of the current political crises gripping the nation. We are ailing as a country but from what I can see all the key players think they have a solution to what bedevils Kenya.

Gen Zs think that they have the monopoly of wisdom. Those in the political opposition shout from rooftops and their high horses with righteous anger.

State apparatchiks rail at sinister forces bent on collapsing Kenya to seize power by unlawful means. Clerics, civil society, the press, and the hoi polloi point an accusing finger at the political elite. Each of these demographics are both right and wrong.

The truth is that no single person has the answer why Kenya is suffering from seemingly sustained political epileptic seizures. But this much is true, as has been throughout history around the world – one finger cannot kill a flea.

No national crisis anywhere has ever been solved by one individual, no matter how gifted. Not even Africa’s political saint Nelson Mandela could end Apartheid alone. What is required is for each of us to climb down from our lofty perches and see, listen, and hear one another. The holier-than-thou harangues of some will be our undoing. Those who claim moral superiority over others forget that when they point at the side opposite, the other four fingers are pointing back at them.

Let me break down what I believe are our Achilles Heels – the immediate triggers of our national cliff-hanger. Our first and most serious national danger is existential, and it’s the youth bulge. We have known for decades that our population pyramid would perilously invert with the youth becoming the most populous demographic.

Stupidly, we have failed to plan for this eventuality. What makes the youth bulge even more volcanic is the fact that many of the youth are educated, but without economic opportunity. This nexus of education and lack of economic belonging has created a train wreck. We are headed for a head-on collision. In fact, it’s happening now. Our lazy political elite has lived high on the hog without planning for its children.

## Create jobs

Our second sin is debt. As we know, borrowing per se has never been a sin. It’s what you do with the monies that you borrow that could be sinful. We have done two mindless things with our borrowing. We have used the money to build shiny objects. I agree that shiny objects are cool.

These are mega projects like airports, rail and road networks, and other infrastructure. The problem is that these important projects don’t really create jobs, especially for the youth. Put crassly, people can’t “eat” roads. What was needed is to borrow to invest in manufacturing, IT, and public works to create sustainable local jobs for our youth and the unemployed. You can’t educate people without an off ramp.

Our third sin is corruption. We construct gaudy infrastructure like the Nairobi Expressway, which only elites can afford for their gas guzzlers. The majority of people in Nairobi use matatu and boda-boda. The Expressway is off limits to them. So, how does it benefit Nairobians? That’s not all. We steal enormous amounts from what we borrow in kickbacks, procurement heists, and other malfeasance. Today, our biggest expenditure is paying off the debt. We have no money to invest in our youth. This is a structural, systemic, and volcanic constraint that’s now exploding in our faces. That’s why both debt and corruption are the most serious security and existential threats to Kenya.

I am not here to lament. On Saba Saba, as the mayhem of the day came to a close, ODM’s Raila Odinga issued a challenge and clarion call to a national palaver. He called it an Intergenerational Conclave for an inclusive and thoughtful dialogue to chart together a shared future out of our current paralysis fuelled by violence and calumny. Political demagogues who lustily want power will stop at nothing including stoking a genocidal cataclysm.

## Resolve urgent national issues

We can’t allow them to succeed. We have seen what happens in other post-colonial states, including many in Africa, when elites and the people don’t talk to resolve urgent national issues. At this stage, talking isn’t an option, but a national imperative.

I’ve seen some folks lambasting Mr Odinga for calling for the conclave and a referendum to settle on solutions. These refuseniks argue that talks in the past have been unproductive and that what’s needed is to implement the 2010 Constitution.

They are both right and wrong. Right because we need to implement the Constitution. Wrong – very wrong – because the implementation of the Constitution has stalled. The conclave is what will give us a kick in the rear to unfreeze and unlock its implementation and end our national lethargy and crises. We must talk – now!
