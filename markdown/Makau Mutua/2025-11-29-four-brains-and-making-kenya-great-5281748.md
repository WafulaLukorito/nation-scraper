The ambitions of every country and state reside in its brains. Both country and state are living organisms. They are born, but they can also die, or atrophy. Like humans they think, aspire, and can prosper, or retard.

They can be healthy, or sickly. Their growth can be robust, or stunt. In one decade, a country can make stunning progress, but during another it can experience demoralising reversals.

In some countries, like China over the last several decades, economic growth and invention can be spectacular. In others, like Somalia, a country can be mired in dystopia. Yet in others, like the United States, they can see unparalleled global dominance. Or like Russia, an unrealised ambition from great potential.

These are the curses, or blessings, of the modern state. Some states are condemned to live in antiquity, and the museums of the horrible, while others are blessed to experience the fortunes and fruits of the human brain. It matters not what the natural endowments a country possesses. No – it’s not the weather, the environment, or what’s underneath a country’s soil. Nyet. Rather, it’s what in a country’s four brains Let’s be clear.

Every country has four brains. Four. Not three. Two, or one. No country has more brains than another. All have four. No more. No less. If you want to assess a country, examine its four brains, and how it uses each of its four brains.

Sit back this Sunday morning and let me peel your eyes, or open your brain. Unlike most animals which toil in the bush, humans are gifted with a brain that has a high organisational capacity. That’s why humans, unlike animals, don’t simply wait for the fate of nature to determine their fortunes. Instead, most humans bend nature to their will, harvesting its bounties.

## Frictional creativity

Of course humans manipulate nature either for good, or ill. The human brain invented nuclear fusion and discovered penicillin. What use each of these great inventions are put to depend on the genius – evil or benign – of the human brain.

One can save humanity from penury, but it can also exterminate, or annihilate, the human race.

What’s my point? Allow me to extrapolate. Each country has four brains. These are brains are, and not in this order – the political brain, the intellectual brain, the elite brain, and the common brain. All these brains need to work in creative tension and competition with one another for a country to harvest greatness.

They need not work in subservient harmony, but in frictional creativity. Each brain must do what it does best without let, or hindrance, from the other brain. But there must be organic interdependence among all the brains for greatness to occur. Some brains are bigger than others. But it’s not the size of the brain that determines its contribution. It’s rather how well it does its job that yields greatness.

The political brain of a county, or state, may not be the biggest factor, but spatially it occupies a commanding plateau in a state’s developmental landscape. What is the political brain? The political brain is the normative edifice that makes policy and steers the ship of state.

It’s essentially composed of the three arms of government in a modern state – the executive, the legislature, and the judiciary. These three pistons are the hardware that make the state function. The state functionality can only take the country as far as this brain will allow.

If this brain is stupid, or dysfunctional, then the state will either fail, become dystopian, or dysfunctional. Its fortunes will rise, or fall, based on the brilliance and ethics of the political brain.

## Political opponents

In Kenya, the head of the political brain is [President William Ruto](https://nation.africa/kenya/people/william-samoei-ruto-3809570). Although the political brain of a state has three heads, the head of the executive is the first among equals in a modern state. That’s why Kenyans must pay close attention to President Ruto when he says that his goal is to lead Kenya towards a first world status.

No Kenyan president has ever dreamt that big. In my view, no Kenyan president has had a political brain as ambitious as President Ruto’s. I have seen some of his political opponents lambast him for dreaming big. These nattering nabobs of negativism don’t understand that President Ruto’s ambitions for Kenya no matter what they think of them are we need to become a great nation.

But the political brain can’t do the job alone. It must be complemented by the intellectual brain. The intellectual brain is the repository of knowledge. It’s found in the university. This is where ideas are brewed. Then the elite brain in business, the professions, and policy feeds from the intellectual brain.

Finally, the common brain – the genius of the hoi polloi – must kick in. The common Kenyan must believe in their greatness and dream big. These brains together can make Kenya great.
