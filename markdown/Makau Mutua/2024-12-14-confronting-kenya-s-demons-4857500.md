On March 25, 2016, Dr David Ndii, now Chair of the President’s Council of Economic Advisors, wrote an erudite yet provocative piece in the *Nation* titled, “[Kenya is a cruel marriage, it’s time we talk divorce](https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/kenya-is-a-cruel-marriage-it-s-time-we-talk-divorce-1183504).”

I am not sure Dr Ndii wants to be reminded about that seminal column. But from where I sit, the intellectual pugilist was right on the money. It’s not rocket science that most post-colonial African states are a work in progress. An experiment in statehood and nationhood. For this reason, Kenya isn’t out of the woods yet. Dr Ndii, by that piece seemed to believe that Kenya as constituted would never be out of the woods.

No one knows the answer to the question Dr Ndii posed. I don’t, and won’t pretend to be a smarter prognosticator than history itself. I know this – we collectively as Kenyans can prevent the worst of Dr Ndii’s dark predictions. We can either rise together, or fall as one in a heap of garbage. What happens to us lies squarely in our hands.

We control our own destiny in spite of the destructive winds of change blowing across our imperilled planet. All countries have their own demons. The biggest American demon is racism. But the USA has other demons too – hatred for the impoverished, its proclivity to be a war machine, and untrammeled individualism and egotism. It’s not alone.

## Evil demons

Kenya has a number of very large and evil demons. Today, I will only deal with the most important one which threatens to sink us. In my view, Kenya’s greatest cancer isn’t dissimilar to that of the United States. Like the US, Kenya’s number one disease is the corrosive power of identity in politics.

Racism bated the US into the pit of enslavement of Black Africans and the catastrophic Civil War that nearly ended the country. But the ugly spectre of racism has never ended in spite of huge strides to address it. It still beckons America towards a death chamber. In Kenya, it’s the tribe, stupid! I use the pejorative word “tribe” even though it has toxic racist connotations.

The colonial state was planted on a bed of tribalism and the doctrine of divide and rule. The White British imperialists created an ethno-state on which they sat atop Arabs, Asians, and Black Africans. What’s more, they pitted these groups against each other and created racial and tribal hierarchies and stereotypes that persist to this day.

These splitist divisions entrenched economic and social strata that in turn influence politics to date. They created many mini-demonic countries in one and in essence no single country for any. They gave us an ogre, an instrumentality bent on the consumption of humans. Our singular task since we kicked out the British in 1964 should’ve been to stitch Kenya together. Sadly, we haven’t.

### Racial divisionism

Rather than heal the wounds of colonialism and create one nation out of many, we’ve gone in the opposite direction. We have exacerbated ethnic and racial divisionism and courted the worst excesses of the human ego. Where we should have developed one political lingua franca, we have insisted on buttressing the 42 tribal colonial district cartography the White Man imposed on us.

Even when we had an opportunity to do something different, we instead reified this colonial map-making. In the 2010 Constitution we created 47 tribal counties on the blueprint of the 42 colonial districts. Shame on us. Why would we, supposedly independent peoples, go back to drink from the racist’s well? Do we lack imagination this badly?

Our politics seem to be irredeemably tribal. We go where the ethnic bigots and barons tell us to go, and we vote as ethnic blocs. Except the 1963 elections – the first democratic poll in Kenya – every one of our elections have been tribal. I know folks tend to single out the 2002 and 2022 elections as an exception to this rule.

That’s bunk. It’s far from the truth. In the 2022 elections, ethnic nations voted as instructed by their kingpins. All the Narc “tribes” voted together as demanded by their leaders. The Kanu “tribes” voted together. While the outcome to remove President Daniel Moi, the dictator, was a success, the election itself was innately undemocratic because voters grouped into tribes.

Similarly, some analysts see the 2022 election as a departure from the tribal norm because DP William Ruto bagged over 80 per cent of the Gikuyu, Embu and Meru Association (Gema) vote. Two flaws in this argument. First, there wasn’t a credible Gema candidate. Second, his opponent was Azimio’s [Raila Odinga](https://nation.africa/kenya/people/raila-amolo-odinga-3809512) who has been political poison to Gema for reasons of ethnic demonisation.

Third, Gema itself proved my point by voting as an ethnic bloc as did the Luo, Kamba, Kalenjin, and Luhya. It’s disingenuous to argue that the 2022 election was issue-based, not ethnic. These elections prove Dr Ndii only partially right. Next week, in Part 2, I will demonstrate how to slay the dragon of tribalism.
