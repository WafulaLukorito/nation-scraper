# Bid to pull Kenya out of ICC an act in futility 

**Author:** Unknown Author  
**Date:** 2011-01-01T14:18:55Z  
**Source URL:** https://nation.africa/kenya/blogs-opinion/opinion/bid-to-pull-kenya-out-of-icc-an-act-in-futility--751296  

---

## What you need to know:

* A big number of the MPs were not serious about the motion. Most of them were simply shedding crocodile tears

*E pluribus unum,* or “Out of many, one” is the dictum on the Seal of the United States. It’s a motto that unites, rather than divides.

I want to urge our MPs to ponder the spirit of this dictum before they take Kenya off the cliff with them. Are we all – whether poor, rich, powerful, or powerless – one country, or not? Are we a country of the powerful against the powerless? Is it “us” against “them”?

I ask the question for one reason. I have never witnessed something as unbelievably callous as the motion by MPs to pull Kenya out of the ICC. I have four reasons why the motion is tomfoolery, an act of futility.

The first reason is consequentialist, by which I mean the effects or consequences of the motion. Parliament has no power to “withdraw” Kenya from a treaty. That’s the preserve of the Executive.

I doubt that President Mwai Kibaki and PM Raila Odinga would be so reckless as to heed a mob of legislators. I bet they won’t risk turning Kenya into a pariah state. Mr Odinga has more to lose than Mr Kibaki. His international credibility as a statesman would evaporate instantly.

Mr Kibaki’s legacy, as the man who gave Kenya a gleaming new Constitution, would be shattered. That’s why I expect the two principals to treat the motion on the ICC with the contempt it deserves.

**Purpose of motion**

The second reason is instrumentalist, by which I mean the purpose of the motion. Whose instrument was the motion, and what was it meant to achieve? I should set the record straight.

I don’t think MPs were serious about the motion. Perhaps there were some diehard supporters of the Ocampo Six.

But most of the MPs were simply shedding crocodile tears. The show of “solidarity” with the six was stage-managed.

Take, for example, my erstwhile comrade Energy minister Kiraitu Murungi. I laughed out loud when I heard him call the ICC “colonialist” and “imperialist”. I know Mr Murungi well. He knows that hypocrisy has its limits. He didn’t mean a word of it.

Why, then, did Mr Murungi and the other MPs pander to the Ocampo Six? Many MPs are beholden to the named suspects. The 2012 political alliances may depend on how ODM and PNU treat “Ocampo’s Victims”.

The KKK alliance, which PNU hopes to use to ride to State House in 2012, may disintegrate if the party abandons Finance minister Uhuru Kenyatta and Eldoret North MP William Ruto.

**The beneficiary**

Even if Mr Kenyatta and Mr Ruto were carted off to The Hague, the PNU hopes to harvest the Kikuyu and Kalenjin votes by claiming that it stood by “their men”. In this scenario, VP Kalonzo becomes the beneficiary of the ICC motion and keeps the KKK alliance together.

I am glad that Mr Odinga has strongly repudiated the ICC motion. It’s pointless to show solidarity with Industrialisation minister Henry Kosgey, ODM’s main man in the Rift Valley. Mr Kosgey is a paper tiger who is not worth the trouble. He cannot save the Kalenjin for Mr Odinga.

My crystal ball tells me that the divorce between Mr Odinga and the Kalenjin is irreversible as long as he is on the outs with Mr Ruto.

The wise course for Mr Odinga is to fully support the ICC, reject the ICC motion, and stand with the people of Kenya who oppose impunity. He is wise not to play tribal math with justice for the victims of post-election violence.

The third reason why the ICC motion is tomfoolery is the “boomerang effect”. This is when you shoot a bullet that ricochets and strikes you back. The motion has irreparably damaged MPs at home and abroad.

At home, Kenyans believe that the MPs would rather protect the alleged perpetrators of the mayhem – killers, looters, and rapists – than the victims. The day the motion passed will live in infamy.

But the motion assures that the geese of the Ocampo Six are truly cooked abroad. The motion convinces the ICC – especially the judges – that Kenya is a cesspool of impunity. That’s why the ICC will be more likely to indict the six and bind them over for trial.

The fourth, and last, reason is that the motion is an act of futility. Why? Because the motion – even if President Kibaki and PM Odinga were to cave in and write to the UN pulling Kenya out of the ICC – would have zero effect on the suspects.

It would take a year from the date of their letter to the UN to withdraw Kenya from the ICC. Besides, the withdrawal would have no effect on ongoing investigations and prosecutions. What’s more, Kenyans could still be indicted by the ICC even if the country was not a party to the treaty.

**Indicted by ICC**

President Omar Bashir of Sudan and his compatriots have been indicted by the ICC even though Sudan is not a party to the ICC statute.  
I want to salute Gichugu MP Martha Karua for standing up to impunity. She was the only one “man” enough to stand up for the “little people”.

I have a warning for our MPs. Don’t play fast and loose with the fate of Kenya. I know your passage of the ICC motion was largely hypocritical, but it greatly damaged our country.

The American motto – *e pluribus unum* – seeks to build a “more perfect union”. I ask you – why do you want to put Kenya asunder?

*Makau Mutua is Dean and SUNY Distinguished Professor at the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School and Chair of the KHRC.*
